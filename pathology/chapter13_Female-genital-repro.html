  • bartholin cyst

    cystic dilation, inflam and obstruction, usu women of reproductive age
    UNILATERAL painful cystic lesion at LOWER VESTIBULE adjacent to vaginal canal

  • condyloma

    warty neoplasm, due to HPV 6/11 or secondary syphilus (STD), koilocytes (raisin nuclei) for HPV, rarely progress to carcinoma

  • lichen sclerosis

    thinning of epidermis, fibrosis/sclerosis of dermis, leukoplakia with parchment skin, postmenopausal women, benign with small inc risk SCC

  • lichen simplex chronicus

    squamous hyperplasia, leukoplakia with thick leathery vulvar skin, due to irritation/scratching, benign with NO inc risk SCC

  • vulvar CA

    from squamous epithelium, rare, leukoplakia (must biopsy), HPV-related due to 16/18 (multiple partners, early intercourse
    young women
    VIN is dysplasia with koilocytic change, disordered cells, nuclear atypia, inc mitotic activity), non-HPV-related is from longterm lichen sclerosis (elderly)

  • extramammary paget disease

    malignant epithelial cells in epidermis, carcinoma-in-situ with no underlying carcinoma
    erythematous, pruritic, ulcerated
    PAS+ keratin+ S100- (compare to melanoma PAS- keratin- S100+)

  • Adenosis

    focal columnar epithelium in upper vagina, failure of squamous to grow upward (lower 1/3 squamous from urogenital sinus, upper 2/3 columnar from mullerian duct), exposed to DES in-utero

  • clear cell adenocarcinoma

    malignant gland prolif with clear cytoplasm, complication of DES adenosis
    DES causes adenosis and abnl uterus in fetus plus inc risk BrCA in mother

  • embryonal rhabdomyosarcoma (sarcoma botryoides)

    malignant mesenchymal prolif of immature skeletal muscle
    bleeding and grape-like mass protruding from vagina/penis
    children <5 y/o
    rhabdomyoblast has cytoplasmic cross-striations, Desmin+, myogenin+

  • vaginal carcinoma

    squamous epithelium carcinoma, usu assn high-risk HPV, VAIN precursor lesion
    lower 1/3 vagina to inguinal LN, upper 2/3 vagina to regional iliac LN

  • cervix

    exocervix= nonkeratinizing squamous, endocervix=single layer columnar
    distinct transformation zone

  • HPV

    STD, DNA virus, infects esp transformation zone, usu eradicated by acute inflam, persistant infx has inc risk for dysplasia (CIN), risk of CIN with high-risk HPV 16/18/31/33 (E6-p53 and E7-Rb)

  • Cervical intraepithelial neoplasia (CIN)

    koilocytic change, disordered maturation, nuclear atypia, inc mitotic activity
    grades based on epithelial involvement (1 <1/3 thickness, 2 <2/3 thickness, 3 CIN1/2 can regress, higher grade more likely to progress to CA

  • cervical CA

    invalsive, middle-aged women
    vaginal bleeding, post-coital bleeding
    key RF is high-risk HPV infx (25-30yr after infx), other RF is smoking and immunodeficiency
    MC is SCC, can get adenoCA
    grows locally to invade anterior uterine wall into bladder (block ureter- hydronephrosis with postrenal failure... MCC death)

  • Pap smear

    gold standard for screening CIN before progression to CA, begins age 21, high grade have hyperchromatic nuclei and high N:C ratio, significant reduction mortality/morbidity, abnl pap confirmed by colposcopy and biopsy
    limitations- inadequate sampling of transformation zone, not good for screening adenoCA

  • HPV immunization

    quadrivalent 6/11/16/18 (condyloma and CIN), 5yr protection, pap smears still necessary because all types not covered

  • Endometrium

    mucosal lining of uterine cavity, hormonally sensitive
    estrogen=growth (prolif phase), progesterone=prep for implantation (secretory phase), loss of progesterone=shedding (menstrual phase)

  • Asherman syndrome

    secondary amenorrhea, loss of basalis (regenerative stem cells of endometrium), from aggressive D&C

  • Anovulatory cycle

    lack of ovulation --> estrogen prolif phase without progesterone-secretory phase
    dysfunctional uterine bleeding in menarche and menopause

  • acute endometritis

    bacterial infx of endometrium, from retained products of conception (nidus for infx)
    fever, abnl uterine bleeding, pelvic pain

  • chronic endometritis

    chronic inflam of endometrium, plasma cells, from retained products of conception/IUB/TB/chronic PID (chlamydia)
    abnl uterine bleeding, pain, infertility

  • endometrial polyp

    hyperplastic protrusion of endometrium, abnl uterine bleeding, side effect of tamoxifen (pro-estrogenic effect on endometrium)

  • endometrioisis

    endometrial glands/stroma OUTSIDE of uterine lining
    due to RETROGRADE MENSTRUATION, metaplastic theory, or lymphatic dissemination theory
    dysmenorrhea and pelvic pain (cycles just like nml endometrium))
    Ovary endometriosis- chocolate cyst (blood debris), most common site
    other sites: uterine ligamets- pelvic pain, pouch of douglas- defecation pain, bladder wall- urination pain, bowel serosa- adhesions, fallopian tube- scarring with inc risk ectopic pregnancy (GUN POWDER NODULES)

  • Endometrial hyperplasia

    hyperplasia of glands, from unopposed estrogen (obesity, PCOD, estrogen tx), POSTMENOPAUSAL UTERINE BLEEDING
    best predictor of progression to CA is cellular atypia

  • Endometrial carcinoma

    malignant prolif of endometrial glands, MC invasive CA of female genital tract, POSTMENOPAUSAL BLEEDING
    Endometrioid- hyperplasia pathway (75%), assn estrogen exposure (early menarche, late menopause, nullparity, infertility with anovulatory cycles, obesity), avg age 60
    Papillary Serous- sporadic pathway (25%), no precursor, atrophic endometrium, avg age 70, psammoma body and p53 mutation, aggressive

  • Leiomyoma/fibroids

    benign, SM prolif from myometrium, MC tumor in females, assn estrogen exposure, premenopausal women, multiple well-defined wholed tumors, enlarge in pregnacy, shrink after menopause
    usually asymptomatic
    can get abnl uterine bleeding, infertility, pelvic mass

  • Leiomyosarcoma

    malignant, SM prolif from myometrium, de novo (not from leiomyomas), postmenopausal women, single lesion with necrosis and hemorrhage

  • Polycystic ovarian dz (PCOD)

    multiple ovarian follicular cysts from hormone imbalance, 5% women of reproductive age, LH:FSH >2, hirsutism from LH-induced angrogen from theca cells, adipose tissue makes estrone --> neg feedback causes dec FSH causing cystic degen of follicles, inc risk endometrial CA
    obese young female with infetility/oligomenorrhea/hirsutism, may have insulin resistance leading to DM2 10-15yr later

  • Ovarian surface epithelial tumors

    MC type ovarian tumor (70%), from coelomic epithelium of ovary --> serous fallopian and mucinous endocervix
    serous has watery fluid, mucinous has mucus-like fluid
    BRCA1 mutation carrier has inc risk for serous carcinoma of fallopian tube and ovary
    present late- poor Px, vague abd sx and compression sx (urinary frequency)
    local spread to peritoneum
    CA-125 marker for tx response and recurrence
    cystadenoma- benign, single cyst with simple flat lining, premenopausal women 30-40y/o
    cystadenocarcinoma- malignant, complex cysts with thick shaggy lining, postmenopausal women 60-70y/o
    borderline tumors- both benign and malignant features, better Px than malignant, metastatic potentia
    Endometrioid tumor- endometrial-like glands, usu malignant, may be due to endometriosis, 15% also have endometrioid endometrial carcinoma
    Brenner tumor- bladder-like epithelium, usu benign

  • Germ cell tumors (GCT)

    2nd MC ovarian tumor (15%), women of reproductive age, mimic tissues produced by germ cells
    cystic teratoma- fetal tissue (skin, hair, bone, cartilage, gut, thyroid), MC GCT, 10% bilateral, benign
    immature neural tissue or somatic malignancy (skin SCC) indicate malignant potentail
    struma ovarii is mostly thyroid tissue
    dysgerminoma- resemble oocyte (large cells with clear cytoplasm and central nuclei), MC malignant GCT, good Px, LDH elevated, testicular=seminoma
    endodermal sinus tumor- mimics yolk sac (serum AFP), MC GCT in children, Schiller-duval bodies (glomeruloid-like structures)
    choriocarcinoma- cyto/syncytiotrophoblasts W/O villi, small hemorrhagic tumor with HEMATOGENOUS spread, high beta-HCG by syncytiotrophoblase causes thecal cysts in ovary, poor response to chemo
    embryonal carcinoma- large primitive cells, aggressive with early mets

  • Sex Cord-stromal tumors

    Granulosa-Theca: produces estrogen (precocious puberty, menorrhagia/metrorrhagia, postmenopause endometrial hyperplasia with uterine bleeding), malignant, minimal risk for mets
    Sertoli-Leydig: Reinke crystals in tubular sertoli and intertubular leydig, produce androgen (hirsutism, virilization)
    fibroma: benign tumor of fibroblasts
    Meigs syndrome= fibroma, pleural effusion, ascites

  • mets to ovaries

    Krukenberg tumor- mucinous, bilateral, from diffuse-type metastatic gastric carcinoma (signet ring cells)
    pseudomyxoma peritonei- massive amounts of mucus in peritoneum, from mucinous appendix tumor

  • ectopic pregnancy

    implantation in site other than uterine wall, MC fallopian tube lumen
    key RF is scarring (PID or endometriosis)
    lower abd pain few weeks after missed period, surgical emergency
    risk of hemotosalpinx and rupture

  • spontaneous abortion

    before 20wk, usu 1st trimester, 1/4 of pregnancies
    vag bleeding, cramping pain, passage of fetal tissue
    mostly due to chrm abnl (trisomy 16)
    also due to hypercoag state (antiphospholipid sydrome), congenital infx, and teratogens during 1st 2wk

  • teratogens

    effect depends on dose/agent/timing
    1st 2 weeks= spontaneous abortion, 3-8wk= organ malformation, 3-9mo= organ hypoplasia
    alcohol- MCC mental retardation, facial abnm, microcephaly
    cocaine- intrauterine growth retardation, placental abruption
    thalidomide- limb defect
    cigarette smoke- intrauterine growth retardation
    isotretinoin- spontaneous abortion, hearing/visual impairment
    tetracyclin- discolored teeth
    warfarin- fetal bleeding
    phenytoin- digit hypoplasia, cleft lip/palat

  • placenta previa

    placental implantation in lower uterine segment overlying cervical os, 3rd trimester bleeding, delivery via C-section

  • placental abruption

    separation of placenta from decidua before delivery of fetus, still birth, 3rd trimester bleeding and fetal insufficiency

  • placenta accreta

    placental implantation into myometrium with little/no decidua, can't deliver placenta, postpartum bleeding, requires hysterectomy

  • pre-eclampsia

    pregnancy-induced HTN/proteinuria/edema in 3rd trimester, fibrinoid necrosis in placenta vessels, resolves with delivery, eclampsia= preeclampsia with seizures
    HELLP= preeclampsia with thrombotic microangiopathy (hemolysis, elevated liver enz, low plt)

  • SIDS

    death of healthy infant (1 mo-1yr), no cause, usu during sleep
    RF= sleeping on stomach, cigarette smoke, prematurity

  • Hydatidiform mole

    abnl conception, swollen edematous villi with trophoblast proliferation, uterus larger than nml, beta-HCG higher than nml, dx in 1st trimester with U/S (no fetal heart sounds, snowstorm appearance), 2nd trimester passage of grape-like masses, tx with suction curettage with beta-HCG monitoring (can lead to choriocarcinoma that is RESPONSIVE TO CHEMOTX)
    partial mole- normal ovum with 2 sperm (69 chr), fetal tissue present, some villi hydropic, focal trophoblast prolif, minimal risk of choriocarcinoma
    complete mole- empty ovum with 2 sperm (46 chr), no fetal tissue, most villi hydropic, diffuse/circumferential trophoblast prolif, 2-3% risk choriocarcinoma
