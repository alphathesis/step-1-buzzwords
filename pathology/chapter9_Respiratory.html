    10% of asthmatics

    adolescent males
    profuse epistaxis

    fever, sore throuat, drooling, dysphagia, muffled voice, inspiratory stridor

  • vocal cord nodule

  • laryngeal papilloma

  • laryngeal carcinoma

    squamous carcinoma from epithelial lining, RF= EtOH and tobacco, can be from papilloma
    hoarseness, cough, stridor

  • pneumonia

    infx of lung parenchyma, normal defense impaired (cough reflex, mucociliary escalalator, mucus plugging)
    fever, chills, productive cough, tachypnea, pleuritic chest pain, dec breath sounds, dullness, inc WBC
    dx with CXR, sputum gram stain and culture
    lobar- consolidation of entire lobe, neurophil exudate in airsacs
    strep pneumo (CAP) or klebsiella (entiric flora aspirated, malnourised/elderly/alcoholic/diabetic, currant jelly sputum, abscess)
    congestion --> red hepatization (exudate, neutrophils, hemorrhage) --> gray hepatization (degrade RBCs) --> resolution (type 2 pneumocytes regenerate)
    bronchopneumonia- scattered patchy consolidation around bronchioles, multifocal and bilateral
    staph aureus- secondary pneumonia, abscess, empyema
    H influenzae- secondary pneumonia and COPD
    psuedamonas aeruginosa- CF pts
    moraxella catarrhalis- CAP and COPD
    legionella pneumophila- CAP, COPD, immunocompromised, water source, silver stain
    interstitial/atypical- inc lung markings, mild URI sx (nonproductive, low fever), empty airsacs, infiltrate in alveolar walls
    mycoplasma pneumoniae- MCC, YA, cause IgM AIHA and erythema multiforme, no gram stain
    chlamydia pneumoniae- YA
    RSV- infants
    CMV- postTXP immunosuppresed
    infuenza- elderly, inc risk for superimposed staph aureus or HIB pneumonia
    coxiella burnetii- high Q fever, farmers/vets (spores on cattle or in cattle placenta), rickettsial, no arthropod vector required, heat-resistant endospores, no skin rask
    aspiration- alcoholics and comotose pts, anaerobic bacteria in oropharynx (bacteroides, fusobacterium, peptococcus), R lower lobe abscess

  • TB (mycobacterium tuberculosis)

    primary- initial exposure, focal caseating necrosis in lower lobe and hilar LN (macs and CD4 t cells), fibrosis and calcification forms GHON COMPLEX, asymptomiatic, positive PPD
    secondary- reactivation due to AIDS or aging, apex of lung (high O2 tension), cavitary foci of caseous necrosis, can lead to miliary or tuberculous bronchopneumonia, caseating granulomas, AFB stain, systemic spread (meningitis, cervical LN, sterile pyuria most common, lumbar vertebrae Pott dz)

  • COPD

    airway obstruction, air trapping, inc TLC, dec FEV1/FVC

  • chronic bronchitis

    productive cough (>3 onths over >2 yrs), assn smoking, bronchial mucinous gland hypertrophy (inc Reid index >50%), cyanosis (blue bloaters) b/c mucus traps CO2, inc risk infx and cor pulmonale (hypoxic vasoconstriction)

  • emphysema

    destruct alveolar airsac (lose elastic recoil, collapse airways during exhalation, air trapping), imbalance of proteases (neutros and macs) and antiproteases (A1AT)
    smoking= excessive inflammation, centriacinar emphysema most severe in upper lobes
    A1AT deficiency= lack of antiprotease, panacinar emphysema most severe in lower lobes
    liver cirrhosis due to misfolded protein accumulation (PAS-positive globules)
    minimal sputum, prolonged expiration with pursed lips (pink puffers) to inc back pressure to open collapsed airways, weight loss, barrel chest, hyposemia and cor pulmonale

  • asthma

    reversible bronchoconstiction due to allergic stimuli
    assn allergic rhinitis, eczema, family Hx atopy
    type 1 HSR (allergen --> TH2 --> IL4 (IgE --> mast cell histamine degranulation and generate LT C4/D4/E4), IL5 (eos- major basic protein), IL10 (inhibit TH1)
    dyspnea and wheezing, productive cough with spiral mucus plugs (curshmann spirals), eos-derived charcot-leyden crystals

  • bronchiectasis

    permanent dilation of bronchioles and bronchi due to loss of airway tone, due to necrotizing inflammation damaging airway walls (CF, kartagenner syndrome, tumor, necrotizing infx, ABPA)
    cough, dyspnea, foul-smelling sputum, hypoxemia with cor pulmonale and secondary AA amyloidosis

  • allergic bronchopulmonary aspergillosis

    hypersensitivity to aspergillus = chronic inflammatory damage, asthma or CF pts

  • restrictive dz

    restricted filling of lung, dec TLC, dec FEV1, inc FEV1/FVC, mostly interstitial dz or chest wall abnl

  • idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis

    interstitium fibrosis, assn cyclical lung injury with TGF-beta inducing fibrosis, secondary from drugs (bleomycins, amiodarone) or radiation must be excluded, subpleural patches with end-stage honeycomb lung

  • pneumoconioses

    chronic occupational exposure to small particles, macs engulf and induce fibrosis
    coal carbon dust- diffuse black lung fibrosis, assn RA (Caplan syndrome), mild exposure causes anthrocosis (carbon-laden macs)
    silicosis- sandblasters, fibrotic nodules in UPPER lobe, silica impains mac phagolysosome fomation, inc risk TB
    berylliosis- aerospace workers, noncaseating granuloms in lung and hilar LN, inc risk lung CA
    asbestosis- construction and shipyard workers, LOWER lung fibrosis, pleura plaques, inc risk lung carcinoma and mesothelioma, asbestos bodies (long golden brown fibers assn with iron)

  • sarcoidosis

    systemic dz, noncaseating granulomas, AA females, CD4/macs response to unknown antigen, stellate inclusions (asteroid bodies) within giant cells
    also uveitis, cutaneous nodules, salivary/lacrimal glands
    dyspnea, cough, inc serum ACE, hypercalcemia (1-alpha-hydroxylase activity of histiocytes), tx with steroids

  • hypersensitivity pneumonitis

    inhaled organic antigen (pigeon breeders lung)
    fever/cough/dyspnea hours after exposure, eosinophil response
    chronic leads to interstitial fibrosis

  • pulm HTN

    atherosclerosis of pulm trunk, SM hypertrophy of pulm arteries, intimal fibrosis, plexiform lesions with long-standing
    leads to RVH or cor pulmonale
    exertional dsypnea and R-CHF
    primary= young female, inactivating mutation of BMPR2 causes vasc SM proliferation
    secondary due to hypoxemia, inc volume with L-CHF, or recurrent PE

  • acute respiratory distress syndrome

    damage to alv-cap interface = fluid leakage = edema and hyaline membranes in alveoli, can be secondary to sepsis/infx/shock/trauma/aspiration/pancreatitis/DIC (neutrophil protease and free radical damage of pneumocytes)
    hypoxemia and cyanosis (thickened diffusion barrier), white-out CXR, tx with PEEP

  • Neonatal RDS

    lack of surfactant (type 2 pneumocytes, phosphatidylcholine/lecithin, dec surface tension = prevent collapse) --> form hyaline membranes
    premature (L:S ratio <2), C-section (lack of stress-induced steroids), maternal DM (fetal insulin = dec surfactant production)
    tachypnea, hypoxemia with cyanosis, ground-glass CXR with diffuse granularity
    inc risk PDA and necrotizing enterocolitis, supplemental O2 causes free radical injury, retinal injury, bronchopulmonary dysplasia

  • Lung cancer

    MCC of cancer mortality
    RF= smoking (polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons and arsenic), radon (uranium in soil, uranium miners), asbestos (shipyard)
    nonspecific sx
    solitary coin lesion on imaging
    apical pancoast tumor can compress brachiocephalic vein (unilateral UE swelling) or symp ganglion (horners- ptosis, miosis, anhidrosis), mediastinal tumor can cause SVC syndrome (head and neck edema)
    can metastasize to adrenal gland

  • small cell carcinoma

    central, male smokers, poorly diff, neuroendocrine, paraneoplastic syndrome (ADH, ACTH) or Eaton-Lambert

  • squamous cell carcinoma

    central, MC in male smokers, keratin pearls and intercellular bridges, PTHrP production causes hypercalcemia

  • adenocarcinoma

    peripheral, nonsmokers and female smokers, glands and mucin

  • large cell carcinoma

    central or peripheral, smokers, poorly diff large cells, poor Px

  • bronchioloalveolar carcinoma

    peripheral, no assn with smoking, ASBESTOS, columnar cells arising from Clara cells, pneumonia-like consolidation, good Px

  • carcinoid tumor

    polyp-like mass in bronchus, no assn smoking, well diff neuroendocrine cells, chromogranin positive, low grade

  • mets to lung

    breast/colon, multiple cannon-ball nodules, more common than primary

  • pneumothorax

    air in pleural space
    spontaneous= YA, rupture of apical bleb, trachea shifts toward collapse
    tension= penetrating chest wall injury, trachea pushed opposite, medical emergency

  • mesothelioma

    malignant neoplasm of mesothelial cells of pleura, ASBESTOS
    recurrent plural effusion, dyspnea, chest pain
    tumor encases lung
