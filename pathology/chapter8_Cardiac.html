  • stable angina

    pain with exertion/stress, due to atherosclerosis of coronaries with >70% stenosis, reversible injury, chest pain <20 min, ST-depression from subendo ischemia, nitroglycerin helps

  • unstable angina

    pain at rest, rupture of atherosclerotic plaque with INCOMPLETE occlusion, reversible injury, ST-depression from subendo ischemia, nitroglycerin helps, can progress to MI

  • prinzmetal angina

    episodic pain, coronary vasospasm, reversible injury, ST-elevation from transmural ischemia, NG and CCBs help

  • MI

    necrosis (irreversible), rupture of atherosclerotic plaque with COMPLETE occlusion, severe/crushing pain >20 min, NG does NOT help, ususally LV
    LAD= anterior wall and anterior septum, RCA= posterior wall, posterior septum, and papillary muscles, LCX= lateral wall
    troponin I more sensitive/specific, CK-MB for reinfarction
    tx- ASA/heparin, O2, nitrates, BBs, ACEi, fibrinolysis/angioplasty
    reperfusion causes calcium influx (contraction band necrosis) and reperfusion injury from free radicals

  • MI- changes over time

    <4 hr= no gross or microscopic change
    4-24hr= dark, coagulative necrosis (no nuclei), arrthymia risk
    1-3 days= yellow, neutrophils, risk of fibrinous pericarditis (only transmural infarct)
    4-7days= yellow, macs, risk of free wall rupture (cardiac tamponade), IVS rupture (shunt), papillary muscle rupture (rt coronary, mitral insufficiency)
    1-3weeks= red border, granulation tissue (fibroblasts, type III collagen, BVs)
    months= white scar, fibrosis (type 1 collagen), risk of aneurysm/mural thrombus/ dressler syndrome (auto antibody to pericardium)

  • sudden cardiac death

    unexpected, no sx or <1 hr after sx, MCC ventricular arrhythmia, acute ischemia (90% have preexisting atherosclerosis), can also be MVP/cardiomyopathy/cocaine

  • CHF- left

    caused by ischemia/HTN/dilated CM/MI/restrictive CM
    causes pulm congestion (dyspnea, PND, orthopnea, crackles, intraalveolar hemorrhage with hemosiderin-laden macs), dec flow to kidneys activates RAA with exacerbates CHF, tx- ACEi

  • CHF- right

    MCC LHF, also cor pulmonale (pulm dz), congestion (JVD, painful HSM, nutmeg liver- cardiac cirrhosis, pitting edema)

  • congenital heart defects

    weeks 3-8, most are sporadic, often cause shunting, L-->R usually asymptomatic but can reverse (Eisenmenger syndrome, RVH, polycythemia, clubbing), R-->L usually cyanotic at birth

  • VSD

    most common defect, assn fetal alcohol syndrome, L--R but can reverse, small defects close spontaneously

  • ASD

    usually ostium secundum (90%), ostium primum assn with down syndrome, L--R with split S2 (delay PV closure), parodoxical emboli

  • PDA

    assn congenital rubella, L--R aorta --> PA, continuous machine-like murmur, leads to Eisenmenger with LE cyanosis, tx- indomethacin (dec PGE = close PDA)

  • tetrology of fallot

    right outflow stenosis + RVH + VSD + aorta overrides VSD
    R-->L shunt (early cyanosis)
    squating in cyanotic spell inc arterial resistance to dec shunt, boot-shaped heart

  • transposition of great vessels

    PA from LV and aorta from RV, assn maternal diabetes, shunt required to allow blood mixing (tx- PGE to keep PDA), causes RVH and LV atrophy

  • truncus arteriosus

    single large vessle (failure to divide), early cyanosis from mixing

  • tricuspid atresis

    hypoplasic RV, ASD cause R-->L shunt

  • coarctation of aorta (infantile)

    assn PDA, distal to aortic arch but proximal to PDA, LE cyanosis, assn Turner syndrome

  • coarctation of aorta (adult)

    distal to aortic arch, HTN in UE and weak pulses in LE, collateral circulation across intercostal arteris (notching of ribs), assn bicuspid aortic valve

  • acute rheumatic fever

    2-3 wk after group A beta-hemolytic strep pharyngitis, molecular mimicry of M protein, ASO or anti-Dnase B, fever/inc ESR, Jones criteria (migratory polyarthritis, MV endocarditis with small vegetations causing regurgitation, myocarditis with Aschoff bodies containing Anitschkow caterpillar-nucleus cells and giant cells, pericarditis, subcutaneous nodules, erythem marginatum on trunk and limbs, sydenham chorea)
    usually resolves but repeat exposure to strep causes relapse of acute rheumatic fever which inc risk for chronic rheumatic heart dz

  • chronic rheumatic heart dz

    repeat strep exposure --> relapse acute fever, valve scarring --> stenosis (fish-mouth), MV thickening of chordae tendineae, AV fusion of commisures, valve damage inc risk for infective endocarditis

  • aortic stenosis

    fibrosis and calcification from wear-and-tear (>60 y/o, biscuspid valve inc risk), can be from chronic rheumatic valve dz (coexisting MV stenosis, fusion of commissures), asymptomatic due to cardiac compensation, systolic ejection click with crescendo-decrescendo murmur
    causes concentric LV hypertrophy/angina and syncope with exercis/microangiopathic hemolytic anemia from calcified valve shearing RBCs

  • aortic regurgitation

    backflow during diastole (early, blowing diastolic murmur), from aortic root dilation (syphilis, aortic dissection) or valve damage (infx endocarditis), inc PP --> bounding water-hammer pulse with pulsating nail bed and head bobbing, causes eccentric LV hypertrophy and dilation

  • MVP

    ballooning of MV into LA during systole (mid-slystolic click with regurg murmur becoming softer with squatting), myxoid degenerationg (ground substance accumulation), assn Marfan/Ehlers-Danlos, risk of infx endocarditis/arrhythmia/MR

  • mitral regurgitation

    reflux during systole (holosystolic blowing murmure that's louder with squatting and expiration)
    due to MVP, LV dilatation, infx endocarditis, ACUTE rheumatic dz, papillary rupture after MI
    causes volume overload and LHF)

  • mitral stenosis

    narrowing of MV, usually due to CHRONIC rheumatic dz, opening snap then diastolic rumble, causes LA dilatation (pulm congestion with alveolar hemorrhage, pulm HTN --> RHF, A fib with mural thrombi)

  • Bacterial Endocarditis

    fever, murmur, janeway lesions (nonpainful on palms/soles), osler nodes (painful on fingers/toes), splinter hemorrhages, anemia of chronic dz (dec Fe, inc Ferritin)
    Strep viridans -low virulence, previously damaged valve from rheumatic heart dz or MVP, small vegetation (damaged endothelium = thrombus... trap bacteria during transient bacteremia), subacute
    staph aureus- high virulence, IV drug users, tricuspid, large vegetations that destroy valve, acute
    staph epidermidis- prosthetic valves
    strep bovis- colorectal carcinoma
    HACEK organisms- neg blood culture

  • non-bacterial endocarditis

    sterile vegetation due to hypercoagulable state or adenocarcinoma, mitral valve regurgitation

  • Libman-sacks endocarditis

    assn SLE, sterile vegetation, MV regurgitation

  • dilated cardiomyopathy

    dilate all 4 chambers, most common, systolic dysfunction --> biventricular CHF, can lead to mitral/tricuspid regurg
    usually idiopathic
    can be genetic (AD), coxsackie A/B myocarditis, EtOH abuse, doxorubicin, pregnancy, hemochromatosis

  • hypertrophic CM

    LVH, due to genetic mutations in sarcomere proteins (AD), diastolic dysfunction, dec CO, sudden death in young athlete due to vent arrhythmia, syncope with exercise due to subaortic hypertrophy of vent septum (functional aortic stenosis), myofiber disarray

  • restrictive CM

    dec compliance = dec filling in diastole
    caused by amyloidosis, sarcoidosis, endocardial fibroelastosis (kids), loeffler syndrom (eosinophilia)
    CHF with low voltage EKG and diminished QRS amplitude

  • myoxma

    Most common primary cardiac tumor in adults, benign mesenchymal, gelatinous, pedunculated mass in LA (can cause syncope with MV obstruction)

  • rhabdomyoma

    most common primary cardiac tumor in kids, benign hemartoma, assn tuberous sclerosis, ventricle

  • mets to the heart

    breast, lung, melanoma, lymphoma
    more common than primary
    pericardium --> pericardial effusion
