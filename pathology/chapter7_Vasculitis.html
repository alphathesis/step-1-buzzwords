  • vessel structure

    intima- endothelial cells on BM, media- SM, Adventitia- CT, vasa vasorum

  • temporal giant cell arteritis

    large vessel, carotid branches, >50 y/o, headache (temporal), visual (opthalmic), jaw claudication
    segmental lesions with giant cells and intimal fibrosis, tx- corticosteroids (risk blindness w/o tx)

  • takayasu arteritis

    large vessel, aortic arch at branch points, <50 y/o, visual and neuro sx, weak/absent pulses in UE, tx- corticosteroids

  • polyarteritis nodosa (PAN)

    medium vessels in multiple organs (NO LUNGS), YA with HTN, abdominal pain with melena, skin lesions, HBsAg, transmural inflammation with fibrinoid necrosis and fibrosis (string of pearls), tx- corticosteroid and cyclophosphamide

  • Kawasaki dz

    medium vessel (coronary), Asian kids <4 y/o with viral sx, coronary artery (thrombosis, MI, aneurysm with rupture), tx- aspirin and IVIG

  • Buerger dz

    medium vessels of digits, heavy smokers, ulceration/gangrene/autoamputation/raynaud

  • Wegener granulomatosis

    small vessel, necrotizing granulomatous, nasopharynx/lungs/kidneys, c-ANCA, tx- cyclophasphamide and steroids

  • Microscopic polyangitis

    small vessel, necrotizing, lung/kidney, NO NASOPHARYNX, NO GRANULOMAS, p-ANCA, tx- steroids and cyclophosphamide

  • Churg-Strauss syndrome

    small vessel, necrotizing granulomatous, lung/heart, EOSINOPHILS, p-ANCA, asthma and peripheral eosinophilia

  • Henoch-Schonlein purpura

    small vessel, children, IgA deposition, usually after URI, palpable purpura on buttocks/legs, hematuria (IgA nephropathy), usually self-limited

  • primary HTN

    unknown etiology, 95% HTN cases
    RF= age, race, obesity, stress, lack activity, high-salt diet

  • secondary HTN

    identifiable etiology, 5% cases, MCC Renal artery stenosis (dec flow --> JGA secretes renin --> ATII causes inc TPR and fluid retention
    unilateral atrophy of affected kidney
    elderly males with atherosclerosis or young females with fibromuscular dysplasia)

  • benign HTN

    mild/mod, clinically silent, vessels and organs damaged over time, leads to hyalin arteriolosclerosis

  • malignant HTN

    >200/120, acute end-organ damage (ARF, headache, papilledema), medical emergency, leads to hyperplastic arteriolosclerosis

  • atherosclerosis

    large/medium vessels (abdominal aorta, coronary, popliteal, internal carotid)
    RF= HTN, XOL, smoking, DM, male, postmenopausal female, genetics
    endothelial damage --> lipid into intima --> fatty streaks from lipid-laden macs --> inflammation and healing (plaque)
    necrotic lipid core with fibromuscular cap
    complications= stenosis --> ischemia, rupture with thrombosis, rupture with embolism, aneurysm

  • hyaline arteriolosclerosis

    small arteriole narrowing, proteins leaking into wall (pink hyaline), due to longterm benign HTN or DM, causes glomerular scarring(arteriolonephrosclerosis) and chronic RF

  • hyperplastic arteriolosclerosis

    hyperplasia of SM, thickening of wall, onion skin appearance, from malignant HTN, can lead to fibrinoid necrosis with hemorrhage, causes acute RF (flea-bitten)

  • mockenberg medial calcific sclerosis

    medium vessels, calcification of media, non-obstructive, incidental finding

  • aortic dissection

    intimal tear with dissection of blood through media, proximal 10cm of aorta (high stress) with pre-existing weakness of media (HTN or CT dz)
    HTN = vasa vasorum hyaline arteriolosclerosis leading to medial ischemia and atrophy
    marfan/ehlers-danlos= CT weakness in media (cystic medial necrosis)
    sharp, tearing pain, pericardial tamponade MCC death
    can obstruct branching arteries

  • thoracic aneurysm

    dilation, tertiary syphillis (endarteritis of vasa vasorum causing atrophy and fibrosis of aorta- tree-bark appearance), can dilate aortic valve root (insufficiency), can compress mediastinal structures

  • abdomina aneurysm

    dilation below renal ateries but above bifurcation, due to atherosclerosis in male smokes >60 y/o, pulsatile mass that grows with time, rupture risk if >5cm (hypoTN, pulsatile, flank pain), can compress ureter

  • hemangioma

    benign tumor of blood vessels, at birth, regresses during childhood, pressing = blanching, skin and liver

  • angiosarcoma

    malignant endothelial cells, skin/breast/liver, liver assn with polyvinyl chroride/arsenic/thorotrast

  • kaposi sarcoma

    low grade malignant endothelial cells, HHV-8, purple patches/plaques/nodules, older eastern european males (localized to skin), AIDS and TXP pts spread early
