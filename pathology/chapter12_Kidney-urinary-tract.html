  • horseshoe kidney

    conjoined kidneys connected at lower pole, located in lower abd, caught on IMA root during ascent

  • renal agenesis

    absent kidney formation, unilateral= hypertrophy and hyperfiltration of existing kidney (risk RF)
    bilateral= oligohydramnios with lung hypoplasia, flat face, low ears, extremity defects (Potter sequence)

  • dysplastic kidney

    noninherited, unilateral enlarged, renal parenchyma (cysts and cartilage)

  • PKD

    inherited, bilateral enlarged, cysts in cortex/medulla
    AR= infants with RF and HTN, newborns with Potter, congenital hepatic fibrosis (portal HTN) and hepatic cysts
    AD= YA with HTN/hematuria/RF, inc renin, APKD1/2 mutation, assn berry aneurysm/hepatic cysts/MVP

  • medullary cystic kidney dz

    inherited, AD, parenchymal fibrosis= shrunken kidneys, cysts in medullary CDs

  • acute RF

    azotemia (inc BUN and Cr) with oliguria
    prerenal azotemia- dec blood flow (HF) --> dec GFR, BUN:Cr >15, FENa<1, Urine osm >500
    postrenal azotemia- obstruction downstream (ureters) --> dec outflow --> dec GFR
    early stage BUN:Cr >15 and urine osm >500
    longterm tubular damage BUN:Cr <15 FENa>2 urine osm <500
    ATN (intrarenal)- injury and necrosis of tubular epithelium, MCC ARF, muddy brown granular casts, BUN:Cr <15, FENa>2, urine osm <500
    ischemia (dec blood supply) = preceded by prerenal azotemia, prox tububle and medullary TALH
    nephrotoxic (aminoglycosides, lead, myoglobinuria, ethylene glycol, radiocontrast dye, urate) = elevated BUN and Cr, hyperkalemia with met acidosis, requires supportive dialysis, 2-3 wk oliguria before recovery
    acute interstitial nephritis- drug induced HSR (intrarenal), NSAIDs/penicillin/diuretics
    oliguria, fever, rash, eos in urine
    may cause renal papillary necrosis
    renal papillary necrosis- gross hematuria and flank pain
    due to chronic analgesis abuse (phenacetin, aspirin), DM, sickle cell, severe acute pyelonephritis, acute interstitial nephritis

  • Nephrotic syndrome

    proteinuria >3.5g/day, hypoalbuminemia (edema), hypogammaglobulinemia (risk infx), hypercoag state (lose ATIII), hyperlipidemia and hyperXOL (fatty casts in urine b/c liver trying to make more prot)
    minimal change dz- MCC in children, idiopathic, assn hodgkin lymphoma, normal biopsy (lipid in prox tubule cells sometimes), foot process effacementon EM (cytokine- permeability factor), negative IF, selective albuminuria, tx-steroids
    focal segmental glomerulosclerosis- MCC in hispanic and AA, idiopathic, assn HIV/heroin/sickle cell
    focal sclerosis, foot process effacement on EM, negative IF, no response to steroids, leads to chronic RF
    membranous nephropathy- MCC in caucasian, idiopathic, assn HBV/HCV/solid tumors/SLE/NSAIDs/penicillamine, thick glomerular BM, granular IF, supepithelial deposits with spike and dome, no steroid response
    membranoproliferative gromerulonephritis- Thick GBM with tram-track, granular IF
    type 1= subendothelial, assn HBV/HCV
    type 2= intramembranous, assn C3 nepritic factor (AutoAB, low C3), poor steroid response
    DM- nonenz glycosylation of vasc BM results in hyaline arteriolosclerosis (leaky), eff>aff so inc GFR and hyperfiltration causese microalbuminuria
    sclerosis of mesangium with Kimmelstiel-Wilson nodules
    ACEi slows progression
    systemic amyloidosis- amyloid deposits in mesangium, apple-green birefringence, Congo red

  • Nephritic syndrome

    glomerular inflammation and bleeding, proteinuria <3.5g/day, oliguria, azotemia, salt retention with periorbital edema and HTN, RBC casts and dysmorphic RBC in urine, biopsy with hypercellular/inflammed glomeruli
    immune-complex deposition activates complement --> C5a attracts neutros --> damage
    poststreptococcal glomerulonephritis: 2-3 wk after group A beta-hemolytic infx (impetigo, pharyngitis), hematuria, oliguria, HTN, periorbital edema, usu children, granular IF, supepithelial humps, supportive Tx
    rapidly progressive glomerulonephritis: nepritic that progresses to RF, crescents in bowman space (fibrin and macs)
    linear IF= goodpasture (Anti-BM AB against type 4 collagen), YA males, hematuria and hemoptysis
    granular IF= PSGN or diffuse prolif GN (subendothelial Ag-AB complex deposition, most common renal dz in SLE)
    neg IF= pauci-immune (cANCA for Wegener granulomatosis
    pANCA for microscopic polyangitis and Churg-Strauss)
    IgA Nephropathy (Berger): IgA deposition in mesangium, MC nephropathy, usu children after mucosal infx, IgA IF, gross/microscopic hematuria with RBC casts
    Alport syndrome: X-linked defect in type 4 collagen, thinning and splitting of GBM, isolated hematuria, sensory hearing loss, ocular disturbance

  • UTI

    usu ascending infx, inc risk in female
    RF= sexual intercourse, urinary stasis, catheter

  • cystitis

    bladder infx
    dysuria, urinary frequency, urgency, suprapubic pain, no systemic sx
    cloudy urine >10 WBC, positive leukocyte esterase (pyuria) and nitrites (bacteria), culture >100K CFU
    80% E.coli, staph saprophyticus in young sexually active women, klebsiella pneumoniae, proteus mirabilis (alkaline urine with ammonia scent), enterococcus faecalis
    sterile pyuria has neg culture (urethritis due to chlamydia or gonorrhoeae)

  • pyelonephritis

    kidney infx
    ascending infx, inc risk with vesicoureteral relux
    fever, flank pain, WBC casts, leukocytosis
    90% E.coli, enterococcus faecalis, klebsiellya

  • chronic pyelonephritis

    interstitial fibrosis and atrophy of tubules, due to repeated acute pyelo, due to vesicoureteral reflux (kids) or obstruction (BPH or cervical CA), cortical scarring with blunted calyces, throidization of kidney, waxy casts

  • nephrolithiasis

    solute precipitation as stone, RF high solute conc and low urine volume, colicky pain with hematuria and unilateral flank tenderness, stone usually passed witin hours
    calcium oxalate/phosphate: MC type in adults, MCC idiopathic hypercalcuria, assn Crohn dz and type 1 RTA, tx HCTZ
    ammonium magnesium phosphote: 2nd MC type, MCC urease-positive infx (proteus vulgaris, klebsiella), due to alkaline urine, staghorn calculi in renal calyces, large stones
    uric acid: radiolucent (don't see on XR)
    RF= hot/arid climate, low urine volume, acidic urine
    MC stone in gout, hyperuricemia (leukemia, myeloprolif) inc risk
    tx- hydration and alkalinize urine (NaHCO3), allopurinol
    cysteine: children, assn cystinuria (genetic defect of tubules), staghorn calculi, tx with hydration and alkalinization

  • chronic RF

    from glomerular/tubular/infam/vasc insults
    MCC are DM/HTN/glomerular dz
    uremia/azotemia (nausea, anorexia, pericarditis, plt dysfunc, encephalopathy, urea crystals in skin), HTN from salt/water retention, hyperkalemia with met acidosis, anemia from dec EPO, hypocalcemia from dec 1-alpha hydroxylation of vit D (also hyperphosphatemia), renal osteodystrophy from secondary hyperPTH, osteomalacia, osteoporosis
    tx with dialysis or txp (dialysis causes cysts with shrunken kidneys --> inc risk RCC)

  • angiomyolipoma

    hemartoma of BVs/SM/adipose, inc frequency in tuberous sclerosis

  • RCC

    malignant epithelial tumor from tubules
    hematuria, palpable mass, flank pain triad
    fever, weight loss, paraneoplastic (EPO, renin, PTHrP, ACTH), rarely L varicocele (block L renal vein = block L spermatic vein)
    yellow mass most commonly cleasr cell
    loss VHL --> inc IGF-1 and inc HIF tf (inc VEGF, PDGF)
    sporadic in males as single upper pole tumor (RF- smoking)
    hereditary in YA as bilateral (VHL dz- AD inactivation, inc risk hemangioblastoma of cerbellum, pheochromocytoma, and RCC)
    renal vein involvement causes hematagenous spread to lung/bone, LN spread to retroperitoneal LNs

  • Wilms tumor

    malignant tumor, blastema (immature mesenchyme) with primitive glomeruli/tubules and stromal cells
    MC malignant renal tumor in kids (~3 y/o)
    large unilateral flank mass with hematuria and HTN (renin)
    90% sporadic
    WAGR syndrome (wilms, aniridia, genital abnl, mental/motor retardation- deletion of WT1 on chr 11)
    Denys-Drash syndrome (wilms, progressive renal dz, male pseudohermaphroditism, WT1 mutation)
    Beckwith Wiedemann syndrome (wilms, neonatal hypoglycemia, muscular hemihypertrophy, organomegaly, WT2 cluster mutations on chr 11 esp IGF-2)

  • urothelial/transitional cell cA

    malignant tumor from urothelial lining, MC type of lower UT CA, usu bladder
    RF= SMOKING, naphthylamine, azo dyes, longterm cyclophosphamide/phenacetin
    older adults, painless hematuria
    flat=high grade, then invades, early p53 mut
    papillary= low grade then high grade then invades
    multicocal and recur (field defect)

  • squamous cell carcinoma

    background of squamous metaplasia
    RF= chronic cystitis in older woman, schistosoma hematobium infx in egyptian male, longterm nephrolithiasis

  • adenocarcinoma

    malignant prolif of glands in bladder
    from urachal remnant (bladder dome), cystitis glandularis (columnar metaplasia) or exstrophy (failure to form caudal anterior wall)
