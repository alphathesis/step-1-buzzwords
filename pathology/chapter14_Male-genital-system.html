  • hypospadias

  • epispadias

    urethra on superior surface of penis, abnl gernital tubercle position, assn bladder exstrophy

  • condyloma acuminatum

    benign warty growth, HPV 6/11= koilocytic change (raisin nuclei)

  • lymphogranuloma venereum

    necrotizing granulomatous inflam of inguinal lymphatics/LNs, STD due to L1-L3 chlamydia trachomatis, heals with fibrosis, perianal causes rectal stricture

  • squamous cell carcinoma

    malignant squamous cells on penile skin, 2/3 from high risk HPV, also lack of circumcision (foreskin is nidus for inflam and irritation)

  • precursor in-situ lesions

    bowen dz- leukoplakia on penile shaft or scrotum
    erythroplasia of Queyrat- erythroplakia on glans
    bowenoid papulosis- multiple reddish papules, younger pts, does NOT progress to invasive CA

  • cryptorchidism

    failure of testicle to descend, MC congenital male repro abnormality, most resolve spontaneously, orchiplexy before 2y/o
    can lead to testicular atrophy with infertility, inc risk for seminoma

  • orchitis

    testicle inflam
    D-K chlamydia trachomatis or neisseria gonorrhoeae in YA with inc risk sterility but libido unaffected (leydig spared)
    e.coli and pseudamonas in elderly
    mumps in teens with risk infertility (no inflam if <10y/o)
    AI orchitis has granulomas of seminiferous tubules

  • testicular torsion

    twisting of spermatic cord, obstruct thin veins, hemorrhagic infarction and congestion, congenital failure of testes to attach to inner scrotum via processus vaginalis
    adolescent with sudeen pain and absent cremasteric reflex

  • varicocele

    L-sided dilation of spermatic vein (drains into L renal vein... L-RCCC assn), scrotal swelling with bag-of-worms

  • hydrocele

    fluid in tunical vaginalis, incomplete closure of processus vaginalis (communication with peritoneal cavity in infants) or lymphatic blockage, transilluminated scrotal swelling

  • Germ cell tumors (GCT)

    MC type of testicular tumor (95%), 15-40y/o
    RF= cryptorchidism, klinefelter
    55% seminoma with good px and good response to radioTx, 45% non-seminoma with early mets
    seminoma- malignant, large cells with clear cytoplasm and central nuclei (like spermatogonia, like ovarian dysgerminoma), may make beta-HCG, good Px, HOMOGENOUS MASS WITHOUT HEMORRHAGE/NECROSIS
    embryonal carcinoma- malignant, immature primitive cells, may produce glands, HEMORRHAGIC MASS WITH NECROSIS, early hematogenous spread, chemoTx causes differentiation into teratoma, inc AFP or beta-HCG
    endodermal sinus tumor- malignant, mimics yolk sac (serum AFP), MC GCT in children, Schiller-duval bodies (glomeruloid-like structures)
    choriocarcinoma- cyto/syncytiotrophoblasts W/O villi, early hematogenous spread, high beta-HCG by syncytiotrophoblast (hyperthyroidism and gynecomastia due to alpha unit similar to FSH/LH/TSH)
    teratoma- malignant, mature fetal tissue, AFP or beta-HCG
    Mixed GCT: MC type of testicular GCT, px based on worst component

  • Sex Cord-stromal tumors

    Leydig cell tumor- usually benign, androgen production (precocious puberty, gynecomastia), Reinke crystals
    Sertoli cell tumor- tubules, clinically silent

  • Lymphoma

    MCC testicular mass in >60y/o, diffuse large B-cell type

  • acute prostitis

    bacterial infx (chlamydia/gonorrhoeae in YA, e.coli/psuedomonas in elderly), dysuria with fever and chills, TENDER BOGGY PROSTATE on DRE, WBC and bacterial culture in secretions

  • chronic prostitis

    chronic inflam, dysuria with pelvic/low back pain, NEGATIVE culture, WBC in secretions

  • BPH

    hyperplasia of stroma and glands, age-related, no risk for cancer, related to DHT (5a-reductase in stromal cells, acts on androgen rcptr to make hyperplastic nodules), central periurethral zone (urinary sx), inc risk bladder diverticula, PSA elevated <10ng/mL due to inc glands (liquifies semen)
    tx with alpha-1 antagonist (terazosin, tamsulosin is 1A specific) or 5a-reductase inhibitor (takes months)

  • prostate adenocarcinoma

    malignant prolif of prostatic glands, MCC in men and 2nd MCC death CA death
    RF= age, AA>caucasian>asian, high sat fat diet
    clinically silent (peripheral posterior region so no urinary sx)
    PSA>10ng/mL
    must biopsy- small invasive glands with prominent nucleoli
    gleeson grading based on architecture (high score=worse px)
    spread to lumbar spin and pelvis= osteoblastic mets with low back pain and inc ALP/PSA/PAP
    prostectomy for localized
    advanced dz tx is leuprolid (continuous GnRH to dec LH/FSH) and flutamide (androgen rcptr competitive antagonist)
