  • leukemia

    acute= blasts (large, immature, punched out nucleoli), chronic=mature, lymphocytic= B/T cell, myeloid= RBC/granulocyte/monocyte/megakaryo, high WBC

  • B-ALL

    positive TdT, CD10/19/20, t12:21 good Px, t9:22 (bcl-abl) poor Px, mostly peds, down syndrome kids after 5 y/o

  • T-ALL

    positive Tdt, CD2-8, no CD10, teens, mediastinal/thymic mass

  • AML

    positive MPO, Auer rods, ApML has t15:17 (RAR disruption) and is tx with ATRA, monocytic lacks MPO and infiltrates gums, megakaryoblastic lacks MPO and affects Down syndrom kids before 5 y/o

  • mycosis fungoides

  • polycythemia vera

  • essential thromocythemia

    JAK2 kinase mutation, granulocytes and plt, inc risk bleeding/thrombosis

  • myelofibrosis

  • follicular lymphoma

    t14:18 (BCL2 overexpression), painless LAD, small B cells CD20+, progression to large B cell lymphoma (enlarging LN), lack tingible body macs in germinal centers, monoclonality

  • mantle cell lymphoma

    t11:14 (cyclin D1 overexpression = G1/S transition), small B cells CD20+, painless LAN, late adulthood

  • marginal zone lymphoma

    small B cells CD20+, assn w/ chronic inflam (hashimoto, sjogren, h. pylori), MALToma

  • burkitt lymphoma

    t8:14 (c-myc oncogene), intmd B cell CD20+, assn w/ EBV, extranodal mass in child/YA, African=jaw sporadic=abdomen, starry-sky (macs=stars)

  • diffuse large B cell lymphoma

    large B cells CD20+, most common NHL, high-grade, late adulthood, ENLARGING LN or mass

  • Hodgkin lymphoma

    reed sternberg cells, CD15/30+, no CD20, B sx, nodular sclerosis most common (enlarging cervical/mediastinal LN in female YA, LN w/ bands of sclerosis and lacunar cells), mixed cell has lots of eos, lympho depleted is most aggressive (elderly, HIV)

  • multiple myeloma

    plasma cells in BM, high serum IL6, bone pain with hyperCa (lytic in vertebrae/skull), M spike from monoclonal IgG/A, inc risk infx, rouleaux formation of RBC (dec charge), primary AL amyloidosis (light chain), proteinuria (bence jones protein)

  • MGUS

    M spike, no other features of multiple myeloma, 1% develop MM

  • waldenstrom macroglobulinemia

    B cell lymphoma with monoclonal IgM production, M spike from IgM, no lytic bone lesions, visual/neuro deficits from hyperviscous serum, retinal hemorrhage/stroke, bleeding

  • Langerhans cell histiocytosis

    letterrer-siwe (malignant, skin rash and cystic skeletal defects, infants <2 y/o, rapidly fatal), eosionophilic granuloma (benign, adolescent, no skin involved), hand-schuller-christian dz (malignant, scalp rash, lytic skull defects, DI, exophthalmos, child >3y/o)
