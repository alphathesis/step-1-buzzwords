  • skin structure

    barrier against env and fluid loss
    top epidermis (stem cell basalis, desmosome spinosum, granules in granulosum, anucleate and keratin corneum), botton dermis (CT, nerve endings, vessels, adnexal hair/glands), melanocytes in stratum basalis

  • melanocytes

    basal layer, derived from neural crest, make melanin in melanosomes using tyrosine and pass melanosomes to keratinocytes

  • atopic/eczamatous dermatitis

    pruritic, erythematous, oozing with vesicles and edema
    face and flexors
    type 1 HSR
    assn allergic rhinitis and asthma
    tx topical steroids

  • contact dermatitis

    pruritic, erythematous, oozing with vesicles and edema
    upon exposure to allergens (poison ivy, nickel, irritants, drugs)
    remove agend and topic steroids

  • acne vulgaris

    comedones (white/blackheads), pustules/pimples, nodules
    adolescents
    chronic inflam of hair follicles and sebaceous glands
    androgen --> inc sebum production, excess keratin --> block follicle --> form comedone
    propionibacterium acnes infx= lipases that break down sebum --> proinflm FAs --> pustule/nodule
    tx with benzoyl peroxide, vit A derivatives

  • psoriasis

    well circumscribed, salmon plaques, silvery scale
    scalp and extensors, sometimes nails
    excessive keratinocyte proliferation
    assn HLA-C, lesions in trauma area with env trigger
    acanthosis (epidermal hyperplasia), parakeratosis (nuclei in stratum corneum), neutrophils in stratum corneum (Munro microabscesses), elongated papillae with thinning epidermis (bleeding under scale= Auspitz sign)
    tx with corticosteroids/PUVA/immuneTx

  • lichen planus

    pruritic, planar, polygonal, purple papules with white lines (Wickham striae)
    writsts, elbows, oral mucosa
    inflam of dermal-epidermal junc with saw-tooth appearance
    assn chronic hep C

  • pemphigus vulgaris

    AI destruction of DESMOSOMES, IgG AB agains desmoglein (type 2 HSR)
    skin and oral BULLAE
    acantholysis of spinosum=suprabasal blisters, basal attached to BM=tombstones, thin-walled bullae that rupture=Nikolsky sign
    fish net IF

  • bullous pemphigoid

    AI destruction of HEMIDESMOSOMES, IgG AB against BP180 of BM
    skin blisters in elderly (no oral involvement)
    TENSE BULLAE that don't rupture
    linear IF

  • dermatitis herpetiformis

    AI IgA deposition in dermal papillae tips
    pruritic GROUPED vessicles and bullae, assn celiac dz- resolve with gluten-free diet

  • erythema multiforme

    targetoid rash and bullae (central necrosis surrounded by erythema)
    MC assn HSV infx, also mycoplasma infx/PCN/sufonamides/SLE/malignancy

  • stevens-johnson syndrome

    erythema multiforme with oral/lip involvement, toxic epidermal necrolysis (severe SJS with diffuse skin sloughing, due to adverse drug rxn)

  • acanthosis nigricans

    epidermal hyperplasia, skin darkening (velvet skin)
    axilla or groin
    assn insulin resistance

  • basal cell CA

    malignant basal cells in epidermis, MC cutaneous malignancy
    RF= UVB damage from prolonged sunlight/albinism/xeroderma pigmentosum
    elevated nodule with central ulceration surrounded by telangiectatic vessels
    pink pearl-like papule
    UPPER LIP
    biopsy= basal cell nodule with PERIPHERAL PALISADING
    tx with excision

  • actinic keratosis (Aks)

    precurson to squamous cell carcinoma, scaly hyperkeratotic plaque
    face/back/neck

  • squamous cell CA

    malignant squamous cells with keratin pearls, RF= UVB damage from prolonged sunlight/albinism/xeroderma pigmentosum, immunosuppression, arsenic, chronic inflam from burn/draining
    ulcerated nodular mass
    LOWER LIP
    tx with excision

  • keratoacanthoma

    well diff SCC that develops rapidly and regresses spontaneously
    cup-shaped tumor filled with keratin debris

  • vitiligo

    AI destruction of melanocytes, localized loss of pigmentation or failure to tan

  • albinism

    congenital lack of pigmentation, tyrosinase defect impairs melanin production
    eyes=ocular, eyes/skin=oculocutaneous
    inc risk SCC/basal CA/melanoma (dec protection against UVB)

  • freckle/ephelis

    small tan/brown macule, darkens with sunlight, inc # MELANOSOMES

  • melasma

    mask-like hyperpigmentation of cheeks, assn pregnancy/oral contraceptive

  • nevus/mole

    benign tumor of melanocytes, flat mucule or raised papule with SYMMETRY, SHARP BORDERS, EVEN COLOR, <6mm
    dysplastic nevus is melanoma precursor
    congenital- present at birth, assn with hair
    acquired- benign nests of melanocytes at dermal-epidermal junc (junctional), MC mole in childrenl grows into dermis (compound)
    intradermal nevus MC in adults

  • Melanoma

    malignant melanocyte tumor
    MCC death from skin CA
    RF= UVB damage, dysplastic nevus syndrome (AD dz with dysplastic nevi)
    mole-like with ASYMMETRY, IRREGULAR BORDERS, NON-UNIFORM CORLOR, >6mm
    2 growth phases- radial along epidermis is low risk of mets, vertical into deep dermis is high risk mets
    depth of extension (Breslow thickness) is most important Px factor
    superficial spreading- MC type, early radial growth, good px
    lentigo maligna melanoma- lentiginous radial growth, good px
    nodular- early vertical growth, poor px
    acral lentiginous- palms/soles, on dark-skinned pts, no UV assn

  • impetigo

    superficial bacterial infx, staph aureus or strep pyogenes, children, erythematous macules --> pustules --> rupture with dry crusted honey-colored serum
    usu on face

  • cellulitis

    deep (dermal and subQ) infx with staph aureus or strep pyogenes
    red tender swollen rash and fever
    RF= surgery, trauma, insect bite
    progression to necrotizing fasciitis

  • necrotizing fasciitis

    necrosis of subcutaneous, infx with anaerobic flesh-eating bacteria, CO2 production = crepitus, surgical emg

  • staphylococcal scalded skin syndrome

    skin sloughing with erythematous rash and fever, due to staph aureus (exfoliative A/B toxins = epidermolysis of stratum granulosum)

  • toxic epidermal necrolysis

    skin separation at dermal-epidermal junction

  • verruca/wart

    flesh colored papules with rough surface, due to HPV (koilocytic change), hands and feet

  • molluscum contagiosum

    firm pink umbilicated papules, due to poxvirus, keratinocytes with cytoplasmic inclusions, MC in children/sexually active adults/immunocompromised
