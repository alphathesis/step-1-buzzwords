  • hyperplasia

    new cells from stem cells- inc # of cells

  • hypertrophy

    gene activation, protein synthesis, organelle produciton- inc size of cells
    can lead to dyplasia --> cancer

  • atrophy

    dec organ size b/c dec # cells (apoptosis) or dec cell size (ubiquiting-proteosome degradation of cytoskeleton)

  • metaplasia

    change in cell type (squamous, columnar, urothelial), from reprogramming of stem cells, reversible
    ex- barrett esophagus, vit A dificiency and keretomalacia, myositis ossificans

  • dysplasia

    disordered growth, precancerous, from pathologic hyperplasia or metaplasia

  • aplasia

    failure cell production during embryogenesis- agenesis

  • hypoplasia

    dec cell production during embryogenesis- small organ (streak ovary in Turners)

  • hypoxia

    low o2 delivery, impair ox-phos, dec ATP production
    due to ischemia (atherosclerosis, Budd-chiari, hypoTN), hypoxemia, or dec O2 binding (CO, methemoglobin)

  • reversible cellular injury

    cellular swelling, loss microvili, membrane blebbing, RER swells, dec prot syn

  • irreversible cellular injury

    membrane damage leading to death, PM --> leakage into serum, mito mem damage --> cytochrome c --> apoptosis, lysosome mem damage --> hydrolytic enzymes

  • Cell death

    Loss of nucleus- pyknosis, karyorrhexis, karyolysis

  • Necrosis

    cell death then inflammation, pathologic never physiologic

  • coagulative necrosis

    tissue firm, proteins coagulate but nuclei disappear, pale wedge infarct for ischemic, red infarction if multiple blood supplies

  • liquefactive necrosis

    enzymatic lysis of cells and prot, brain, abscess, pancreatitis

  • gangrenous necrosis

    mummified tissue, lower limb and GI, superimposed infx = wet gangrene (liquefactive0

  • caseous necrosis

    soft and friable tissue, combined coagulative and liquefactive, granulomatous inflam (tuberculous or fungal infx)

  • fat necrosis

    chalky white calcium deposits, trauma to fat or pancreatitis (lipase), saponification (dystrophic calcification- nml serum Ca)

  • fibrinoid necrosis

    blood vessel wall, leaking proteins (fibrin)= bright pink stain, malignant HTN and vasculitis

  • apoptosis

    energy dpdt, programmed, cell shrinks and cytoplasm eosinophilic, caspase cascade activated by cyt C (dec BCL2), extrinsic path- FASL binds CD95 and TNF activates caspase, T cell mediated- perforin, ganzyme

  • free radical injury

    generate in ox-phos, pathologic- ionizing radiation (h2o to OH-), inflammation (NADPH oxidase), metals (Cu, Fe), drugs (P450)
    peroxidize lipids and oxidize DNA/proteins
    antioxidants (SOD for O2-, catalase for H2O2, glutathione peroxidase) - CCl4 -> CCl3 (dec apolipoprot = fatty liver)
    reperfusion injury

  • amyloidosis

    beta-pleated, congo red stain, apple-green birefringence
    systemic- nephrotic syndrome, restrictive cardiomyopathy, tongue enlargement
    primary= AL amyloid, secondary= AA amyloid (SAA -> AA, inc SAA in chronic inflam/malignancy/FMF)
    localized- senile cardiac (non-mut transthyretin), familial amyloid cardiomyobathy (mutated transthyretin), type II DM (amylin), alzheimer (A-beta amyloid), dialysis-assn (Beta 2 microglobulin in joints), medulary carcinoma of thyroid (calcitonin)
