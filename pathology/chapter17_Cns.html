  • Neural tube defect

    incomplete closure of neural tube, neural plate invaginates early to form neural tube (wall=CNS tissue, lumen=ventricles and SC canal, neural crest=PNS), defects assn low folate levels, detect by high AFP
    anencephaly- absence of skull and brain, disrupt cranial end, frog-like appearance, maternal polyhydramnios (impaired fetal swallowing)
    spina bifida- failure of posterior verterbral arch closure, distrupt caudal end
    oculta= dimple or hair patch
    meningocele= meninges protrude
    meningomyelocele= meninges and SC protrude

  • cerebral aqueduct stenosis

    congenital stenosis btwn 3rd and 4th ventricle, CSF accumulation, hydrocephalus (enlarging head circumference from ventricle dilation and cranial suture lines not fused)

  • CSF flow

    produced by choroid plexus
    lateral vent --> IV foramen of monro --> 3rd vent --> cerebral aqueduct --> 4th vent --> foramina of Magendie and Luschka --> subarachnoid space

  • Dandy-walker malformation

    congenital failure of cerebellar vermis development, dilated 4th ventricle in posterior fossa, absent cerebellum, hydrocephalus

  • Type 2 arnorld-chiari malformation

    congenital downward displacement of cerebellar vermis and tonsils through foramen magnum, obstruction of CSF=hydrocephalus, assn meningomyelocele and syringomyelia
    spinothalamic (pain/temp): DRG --> posterior horn (cross in anterior white commisure, ascend in spinothalamic tract) --> Thalamus --> cortex
    Dorscal column/ Medial Lemniscus (pressure, touch, vibration, proprioception): DRG (to medulla via dorsal column) --> Medulla Nucleus Gracilis (LE)/Cuneatus (UE) (cross in medulla, ascend in medial lemniscus) --> thalamus --> cortex
    Lateral corticospinal (voluntary movement): pyramidal cortex (cross in medullary pyramid) --> anterior motor horn --> muscle
    Hypothalamospinal (symp input of face): hypothalamus --> lateral horn at T1 --> superior cervical ganglion --> eyelids, pupil, face skin

  • Syringomyelia

    cystic degeneration of SC, assn trauma or arnold-chiari
    C8-T1
    UE SENSORY LOSS PAIN/TEMP, spare fine touch and position
    affect anterior white commisure, spare dorsal column
    syrinx expansion= muscle atrophy/weakness with dec tone and impaired reflexes (damage LMN), horner syndrome from lateral horn hypothalamospinal tract damage (ptsosis, miosis, anhidrosis)

  • poliomyelitis

    poliovirus infx, anterior motor horn, LMN damage sx (flaccid paralysis, muscle atrophy, dec tone, dec reflexes, neg babinski)

  • Werdnig-Hoffman dz

    inherited degen of anterior motor horn (LMN damage), AR, floppy baby, death within few years

  • amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS, Lou Gehrigs)

    UMN and LMN degen, corticospinal tract
    anterior motor neuron degen - LMN signs
    lateral corticospinal tract degen- UMN signs (spastic paralysis, hyperreflexia, inc tone, pos babinski)
    atrophy and weakness of hands is early sign (NO SENSORY LOSS)
    most sporadic, middle-age adults
    assn SOD1 mutation in familial (zinc-copper, free radical injury)

  • Friedreich ataxia

    degen of cerebellum (ataxia) and SC (loss of vibration/proprioception, LE muscle weakness, loss DTR)
    ARR, GAA trinucleotide expansion of frataxin gene (iron buildup= free radical damage)
    early childhood
    assn hypertrophic CM

  • Meningitis

    inflam of leptomeninges (pia and arachnoid), MC due to infx
    triad- headache, nuchal rigidity, fever
    photophobia, vomiting, altered mental status
    dx with lumbar puncture (btwn L4-L5 at level of iliac crest)
    death from herniation after cerebral edema
    fibrosis --> hydrocephalus, hearing loss, seizures
    Bacterial- GBS/E.coli/Listeria in neonates, N.meningitidis in kids/teens, Strep pneumo in adults/elderly, HIB in nonvax infants
    neutrophils with dec glucose in CSF, gram stain to identify
    Viral- Coxsackie virus (kids, fecal-oral), lymphocytes and nml glucose in CSF
    fungal- immunocompromised pts, lymphocytes and dec glucose in CSF

  • cerebrovascular dz

    85% ischemia, 15% hemorrhage
    neurons dependent on serum glucose and suseptible to ischemia, necrosis within 3-5 min

  • global cerebral ischemia

    low perfusion (atherosclerosis), acute dec in flow (cardiogenic shock), chronic hypoxia (anemia), repeated hypoglycemia (insulinoma)
    mild= transient confusion with promb recovery
    moderate= infarct in watershed areas (btwn anterior and middle cerebral arter zones)
    pyramidal cerebral cortex (3,5,6)=laminar necrosis, pyramidal hippocampus (temporal lobe)= long term memory, cerebellum purkinje layer= sensory and motor integration
    severe= diffuse necrosis, vegetative state survival

  • ischemic stroke

    transient ischemic attack= <24hr Sx, stroke= deficits >24hr
    liquefactive necrosis (12hr red neurons, 24hr necrosis, 1-3d neutrophils, 4-7d microglia, 2-3wk gliosis), form fluid-filled cystic space surrounded by gliosis
    thrombotic stroke- rupture of atherosclerotic plaque, atherosclerosis at branch points (internal carotid bifurcation, middle cerebral art), pale infarc at periphery of cortex
    embolic stroke- thromboemboli, MC source is LH atrial fib, usu middle cerebral art, hemorrhagic infarct at periphery of cortex (reperfusion)
    lacunar stroke- from hyaline arteriolosclerosis due to HTN, lenticulostriate vessels, small cystic infarctions
    internal capsule= purely motor, thalamus= purely sensory

  • intracerebral hemorrhage

    bleeding into parenchyma, CHARCOT-BOUCHARD MICROANEURYSMS OF LENTICULOSTRIATE VESSELS (due to HTN), MC site is basal ganglia
    severe headache, N/V, coma

  • subarachnoid hemorrhage

    bleeding into subarachnoid space
    sudden horrific headache with nuchal rigidity
    lumbar puncture shows xanthochromia (bilirubin breakdown)
    85% RUPTURE OF BERRY ANEURYSM (anterior communicating artery branch point, assn Marfan and ADPKD, thin-walled without media layer), also from anticoagulated state

  • Epidural hematoma

    between dura and skull, fracture of temporal bone/pterion with middle meningeal artery rupture, CT shows lens-shaped lesion, lucid interval before neuro sx, herniation is lethal

  • subdural hematoma

    blood underneath dura covering brain surface, tearing of briding viens btwn dura and arachnoid (trauma, shaken baby), CT shows crescent-shaped lesion, progressive neuro sx, inc rate in elderly from cerebral atrophy that stretches veins, herniation is lethal

  • Herniation

    displacement of brain drue to mass effect or inc intracranial pressure
    tonsilar- cerebellar tonsils into foramen magnum, compress brain stem, cardiopulmonary arrest
    subfalcine- displace cingulate gyrus under falx cerbri, compress anterior cerebral artery- infarction
    uncal- temporal lobe uncus under tentorium cerebelli, compress CN3 (oculomotor- eye down and out, dilated pupil), compress posterior cerebral atery- occipital lobe infaction (contralateral homonymous hemianopsia), rupture paramedian artery- duret brainstem hemorrhage

  • Myelination

    improves speed and efficiency of conduction, oligodendrocytes in CNS, schwann cells in PNS

  • leukodystrophies

    inherited mutation in enzymes for myelin production/maintenance
    metachromatic leukodystrophy- AR arylsulfatase deficiency, MC, sulfatides not degraded (accumulate in lysosome of oligos), lysosomal storage dz
    Krabbe dz- AR galactocerebrosidase deficiency, galactocerebroside accumulates in macs
    adrenoleukodystrophy- X-linked defect in LCFA co-A addition, accumulate FA --> damage adrenal glands and brain white matter

  • Multiple sclerosis

    AI oligos and CNS myelin destruction, MC chronic CNS dz in YA, women, assn HLA-DR2, regions away from equator
    relapsing neuro deficits with remission periods (multiple lesions in time and space)
    unilateral blurred vision, vertigo and scanning speech, internuclear ophthalmoplegia (no contralateral adduction), hemiparesis or unilateral loss sensation, LE loss sensation or weakness, bowel/bladder/sexual dysfunction
    MRI shows plaques, lumbar puncture shows lymphocytes, inc IGs with oligoclonal IgG bands, myelin basic protein
    gray appearing plaques in white matter
    tx of acute with high dose steroids, tx longterm with interferon-beta to slow progression of dz

  • subacute sclerosing panencephalitis

    slowly progressing, persistant infx of brain by measles virus, infx in infancy but neuro signs during childhood, viral inclusions within neurons and oligos

  • progressive multifocal leukoencephalopathy

    JC virus infx of oligos, immunosuppresion causes reactivation, rapidly progressing sx (visual loss, weakness, dementia)

  • central pontine myelinosis

    focal demyelination of pons (anterior brainstem), due to rapid IV correction of hyponatremia in severely malnourished pts, acute bilateral paralysis with blinking intact (locked in syndrome)

  • degenerative disorders

    loss of neurons in gray mater, accumulation of protein damages neurons, cortex=dementia, brainstem/basal ganglia= movement disorders

  • alzheimer disease

    MCC dementia, cortex degeneration, dec ACh
    slow-onset memory loss, progressive disorientation, loss of learned skills and language, behav/personality change, infx is MCC death
    95% sporadic, risk inc with age (double every 5yr after 60)
    E4 allele of APOE= inc risk, E2 allele=dec risk
    early onset in familial (presenilin 1/2 mutation) and down syndrome (APP on chr 21)
    cerebral atrophy with narrow gyri and wide sulci, dilated ventricles (hydrocephalus ex vacuo), neuritic plaques of A-beta amyloid (APP) and may deposit in vessels, neurofibrillary tangles of hyperphosphorylated tau protein (MT-assn)
    clinical dx of exclusion

  • vascular dementia

    2nd MCC dementia, multifocal infarction/injury due to HTN/atherosclerosisi/vasculitis

  • Pick dz

    degen of frontal and temporal cortex (spare parietal and occipital lobes), round aggregates of tau protein (Pick bodies) in neurons, behav and language sx

  • parkinson dz

    loss of dopaminergic neurons in SN of basal ganglia, assn MPTP exposure, TRAP sx (resting tremor, cogwheel rigidity, akinesia/bradykinesia, postural instability/shuffling gait)
    loss of pigmented neurons in SN, round eosinophilic inclusions of alpha synuclein in neurons (Lewy bodies)
    dementia is late dz feature

  • Lewy body dementia

    early-onset dementia
    hallucinations and parkinsonian features
    cortical lewy bodies

  • Huntington dz

    degen of GABAergic neurons in caudate nucleus of basal ganglia
    AD Chr 4 CAG trinucleotide repeat in huntingtin gene, expansion in spermatogenesis causes anticipation
    chorea progressing to dementia, avg age 40
    suicide common cause of death
    athetosis=slow snake-like movement of fingers

  • normal pressure hydrocephalus

    inc CSF causes dilated ventricles, can cause dementia
    triad- urinary incontinence, gait instability, dementia (wet, whacky, wobbly)
    arachnoid absorption impairs
    lumbar puncture improves sx

  • spongiform encephalopathy

    degeneration due to prion dz, beta-pleated confirmation of PrPsc (sporadic, inherited, transmitted)- not degradable, converts normal PrPc to PrPsc (viscious cycle)
    intracellular vacuoles in neurons and glial cells
    CJD- MC, sporadic or txp (GH or corneal), rapidly progressive dementia with ataxia and startle myoclonus, periodic sharp waves on EEG, death <1yr
    variant CJD- madcow, younger pts
    familial fatal insomnia- inherited, severe insomnia, exaggerated startle response

  • mets to brain

    multiple, well-circumscribed, edema, at gray-white junction, from lung/breast/kidney

  • Primary brain tumors

    adult=supratentorial, children=infratentorial
    locally destructive, rarely metastasize

  • glioblastoma multiforme

    MC malignant in adults, malignant high-grade, astrocytes, cerebral hemisphere, crosses corpus callosum (butterfly lesion)
    biopsy shows pseudopalisading (necrosis surrounded by tumor cells), GFAP+, poor px

  • Meningioma

    MC benign in adults, arachnoid cells, women
    seizures, doesn't invade cortex
    round mass attached to dura
    biosy shows whorled pattern with psammoma bodies

  • Schwannoma

    benign, schwann cells, MC CN8 at cerebellopontine angle (loss hearing and tinnitus), S-100+, bilateral in NF type 2

  • oligodendroglioma

    malignant, oligos, calcified tumor in white matter (usu frontal lobe), seizures
    biopsy shows fried-egg cells

  • pilocytic astrocytoma

    MC benign in kids, cerebellum, astrocytes, cystic lesion with mural nodule
    biopsy shows rosenthal fibers and eosinophilic granular bodies, GFAP+

  • medulloblastoma

    malignant, children, from granular cells of cerebellum (neuroectoderm)
    biopsy shows small round blue cells, homer-wright rosettes
    spreads via CSF, drop mets to cauda equina, poor px

  • ependymoma

    malignant, children, ependymal cells in 4th ventricle, hydrocephalus
    biopsy shows perivascular pseudorosettes

  • craniopharyngioma

    benign, child or YA, from epithelial remnants of Rathke's pouch, supratentorial, may compress optic chiasm (bitemporal hemianopsia), recur after resection
