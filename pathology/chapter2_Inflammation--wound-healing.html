  • acute inflammation

    Innate, immediate, neutros, edema
    TLR (PAMPs, CD14, NF-kB), AA metabolites (COX- PG, 5-lipox LT), Mast cells (tissue trauma, C3a/5a, IgE crosslinking -> release preformed histamine, produce AA metabolites/LT), complement (classical C1 with IgG/M C4b2b, MBL C4b2b, alternative C3bBb -> all to C3b -> C5b... opsonization or MAC with C6-9
    C3a/5a analphylatoxins, C5a neuto chemotaxis, MAC lyses), Hageman factor XII (coagulation, kinin system)

  • Leukotrienes

    LTB4= neutro attractant
    LTC/LTD/LTE4= vasoconstrict, bronchospasm, inc vasc perm (contract pericytes to open space btwn endothelial cells)

  • Histamine

    Artriole vasodil, post capillary venule vasc perm

  • Neutrophil infiltration

    migration, rolling (selectins bind sialyl Lewis X), adhesion (ICAM bind integrins), transmigration, phagocytosis, oxidative burst (NADPH oxidase makes O2- -> superoxide dismutase makes H2O2 --> MPO makes HOCl)

  • Leukocyte adhesion deficiency

    AR defect CD18 (integrin)
    delay umbilical cord separation, inc circulating neutros, recurrent bacterial infx without pus

  • Chediak-Higashi

    AR protein trafficking defect, impaired phagolysosome formation
    inc pyrogenic infx, neutropenia from intramedullary death, giant granules in leukocytes, defective platelets, albinism, peripheral neuropathy

  • Chronic granulomatous dz

    X-linked/AR NADPH oxidase defect, recurrent infx with catalase positive b/c no H2O2 for MPO (staph aureus, pseudomonas cepacia, serratia, nocardia, aspergillus), nitroblue tetrazolium test confirms (colorless)

  • MPO deficiency

    Defective H2O2 -> HOCl, inc risk candida infx, nitroblue tetrazolium test nml, most pts asymptomatic

  • Macrophages

    predominate after neutro, peak 2-3days, phagocytosis, lysozyme for O2-indp killing
    resolution via IL10 and TGFb, continue acute inflame via IL8 to recruit neutros, form abscess, chronic inflam via CD4 activation with MHCII/IL12

  • Eosinophils

    Major basic protein for O2-indp killing

  • T cells

    Extracellular Ag= CD4-MHCII... APC B7-CD28 or Bcell-CD40L, TH1=IFNg or IL2, TH2=IL4/5/13 - Intracellular Ag= CD8-MHCI... IL2, perforin/granzyme, FasL for extrinsic apoptosis

  • B cells

    CD40r for CD4 T cell interaction, MHCII
    IL4=IgE, IL5=IgA, IFNg=IgG

  • granulomatous inflammation

    granuloma (epitheloid histiocytes surrounded by giant cells and rim of lymphocytes), noncaseating don't have central necrosis, caseating have central necrosis (TB, fungal infx), mac MHCII to CD 4... IL12->TH1=IFN-gamma->epitheliod histiocytes and giant cells

  • Digeorge syndrome

    failure 3rd/4th pharyngeal pouch, 22q11 mut, T-cell deficiency (athymic), hypocalcemia

  • SCID

    defective cell-mediated and humoral, dec T and B
    due to cytokine receptor defects, ADA deficiency, MHCII deficiency
    fungal/viral/bact/protozoal infx, opportunistic inx, no live vax

  • X-linked aggama-globulinemia

    disordered B-cell maturation, bruton tyrosine kinase mutation, presents after 6mo with recurrent bact/enterovirus/giardia infx, avoid live vax

  • CVID

    low IG from B/T cell defects, late childhood bact/enterovirus/giardia infx, inc risk AI and lymphoma

  • IgA deficiency

    low IgA, most common, inc risk mucosal infx

  • hyper-IgM

    mutated CD40L on TH or CD40 on B, no class switching, low IgA/G/E --> recurrent pyogenic infx at mucosal sites, poor opsonization

  • wiskott-aldrich

    x-linked WASP mutation, thrombocytopenia/eczema/recurrent infx

  • complement deficiency

    neisseria infx (C5-9) - C1 inhibitor deficiency = hereditary andioedema (periorital and mucosal edema from overactivation of complement causing vasodilation and vasc perm)

  • AI dz

    loss of self tolerance (self-reactive T cells eliminated in thymus medulla- AIRE or become anergic- ALPS), reg T cells (CD4+, CD25+, CTLA4, FOXP3+) make IL10 and TGFb (CD25/IL2r polymorphism=MS/DM1, FOXP3 mut=IPEX), women of childbearing age, assn HLAB27 and PTPN22 polymorphism
    relapses and remissions

  • SLE

    Type 3 HSR due to UV damage and uncleared apoptotis debris
    malar rash, oral ulcers, arthritis, serositis, ANA (sensitive), anti-dsDNA or anti-smith (sensitive, specific), antiphospholipd (hypercoagulable, false RPR, not specific), antihistone (drug-induced)

  • Sjogren

    AI destruction of lacrimal and salivary, Type 4 HSR with fibrosis, Dry, primary=sicca syndrome, ANA and anti-SSA/Ro anti-SSB/La, inc risk B cell marginal lymphoma

  • scleroderma

    sclerosis, fibroblasts-collagen, limited-CREST on hands and face (calcinosis, anti-centromere, raynaud, esophageal dysmotility, sclerodactyly, telangiectasia), diffuse (vessels, GI, lungs, kidneys)
    anti topoisomerase I (anti-SCL70)

  • mixed CT dz

    antibodies to U1 ribonucleoprotein

  • Collagen

    Lysine/proline hydroxylation requires vit C, disulfide bond and procollagen formed inside cell, procollagen secreted, ends cleaved, lysyl oxidase requires copper to cross-link
    type 1 strong (skin, bone, tendon), type 2 in cartilage, type 3 in granulation tissue/keloids, type 4 in BM

  • wound healing

    granulation tissue- type 3 collagen, caps, myofibroblast (contraction)
    scar- type 1 collagen, collagenase needs zinc to remove type 3
    TGFa is epi and fibro GF, TGFb is fibro GF, PDGF is epi and fibro GF, FGF for angiogenesis, VEGF for angiogenesis
    primary intention- minimal scar, secondary intention- contract and scar
    delayed wound healing from staph aureus infx, vit C/copper/zinc deficiency
    dehiscence is rupture
    keloid is excess type 3 collagen in AA
