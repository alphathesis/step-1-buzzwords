  • anular pancreas

    developmental malformation, risk duodenal obstruction

  • acute pancreatitis

    inflam/hemorrhage, autodigestion by enzymes, premature trypsin activation, liquifactive and fat necrosis, MCC EtOH and gallstones, also due to trauma (seat belt injury)/hypercalcemia/hyperlipidemia/scorpion sting/mumps/posteriour duodenal ulcer rupture
    epigastric pain radiating to back, N/V, periumbilical hemorrhage, inc serum lipase/amylase, hypocalcemia (Ca used in fat saponification)
    can lead to shock, pseudocyst, abscess (E.coli), DIC, ARDS

  • chronic pancreatitis

    fibrosis of parenchyma from recurrent acute, MCC EtOH and CF
    epigastric pain radiating to back, panc insufficiency (malabsorption, steatorrhea), dystrophic calcification, duct dilatation, secondary DM due to islet destruction, inc risk carcinoma

  • pancreatic carcinoma

    adenocarcinoma from ducts, elderly, RF smoking and chronic panc
    pain, weight loss, obstructive jaundice with pale stools and palpable GB (pancreas head- block bile flow), secondary DM if body/tail of panc, migratory thrombophlebitis (Trousseau sign- swelling, erythema, tenderness in extremities), CA19-9 tumor marker
    whipple procedure to remove head/nec of panc with prox duodenum and GB

  • biliary atresia

    failure to form extrahepatic tree, biliary obstruction within first 2 mo, jaundice (conj bilirubin in blood), progress to cirrhosis

  • gallstones/cholelithiasis

    precipitatin of XOL/bilirubin, due to supersaturation or dec phospolipids/bile acids or stasis, usually asymptomatic
    can lead to biliary colic, cholecystitis, ascending cholangitis, gallstone ileus, GB CA

  • cholesterol stones (yellow)

    MC type, radiolucent
    RF= fat 40 y/o female (estrogen), clofibrate, native american, crohn dz, cirrhosis

  • bilirubin (pigmented) stones

    radiopaque
    RF= EVH and biliary tract infx (ascaris lumbricoides from poor sanitation, clonorchis sinesis in Asia and risk for stones/cholangitis/cholangiosarcoma)

  • biliary colic

    waxing and waning RUQ pain, due to GB contration against stone in cystic duct, common bile duct obstruction can cause acute pancreatitis or obstructive jaundice

  • acute cholecystitis

    acute inflam of GB wall, stone in cystic duct --> dilatation with pressure ischemia and E.coli overgrowth
    RUQ pain radiates to R scapula, inc serum ALP

  • chronic cholecystitis

    chronic inflam of GB, due to irritation from longterm cholelithiasis, herniation of GB mucosa into muscular wall (Rokitansky-Aschoff sinus), vague RUQ pain after eating, porcelain GB (chronic inflam, fibrosis, dystrophic calcification - inc risk CA)

  • ascending cholangitis

    bacterial infx of bile ducts, enteric gram-neg, sepsis/jaundice/pain, inc risk with choledocholithiasis

  • gallstone ileus

    stone enters and obstructs small bowel, cholecystitis with fistula formation

  • GB carcinoma

    adenocarcinoma from epithelum, major RF is stones and porcelain GB, chocystitis in elderly woman, poor px

  • bilirubin metabolism

    protoporphyrin from heme --> UCB in spleen --> carried by albumin to liver --> conj by hepatocyte UGT --> bile canaliculi to form bile --> store bile in GB --> release into intestine --> urobilinogen by flora --> urobilin (reabs into blood and into urine) and stercobilin (stool)

  • jaundice

    yellow discoloration, earliest sx is scleral icterus, inc serum bilirubin >2.5, disturbances in metabolism
    EVH- inc UCB, dark urine due to inc urine urobilinogen, inc risk pigmented stones (inc CB in bile)
    physiologic jaundice of newborn- low UGT activity, inc UCB- fat soluble deposits in basal ganglia (kernicterus), tx with phototherapy (make UCB water soluble so excrete in urine)
    Gilbert syndrome- AR low UGT activity, inc UCB, jaundice during stress
    Crigler-Najjar syndrome- UGT absence, inc UCB in fetus, fatal kernicterus
    Dubin-Johnson syndrome- AR canalicular txp protein deficiency, inc CB, pitch-black liver from CB
    Rotor syndrome- similar to Dubin-Johnson but no liver discoloration
    Biliary tract obstruction (obstructive jaundice)- assn gallstones/panc CA/cholangiocarcinoma/parasites/liver fluke, inc CB (dark urine), dec urine urobilinogen (dec bile), inc ALP, pale stool (dec bile), pruritus from plasma bile acids, hyperXOL with xanthomas, steatorrhea with malabsorption
    viral hepatitis- distrupt hepatocytes and bile ductules, inc CB and UCB, dark urine from inc CB, urine urobininogen nml/dec

  • Viral hepatitis

    inflam of liver parenchyma from hepatitis virus/EBV/CMV
    acute= jaundice, dark urine, fever, malaise, nausea, inc liver enz ALT>AST, inflam of lobules and portal tracts (apoptosis of hepatocytes), <6 mo
    chronic= >6 mo, inflam of portal tract, risk of cirrhosis
    HAV- fecal oral, travel, only acute, IgM is active infx, IgG is protective (prior infx or immunization)
    HEV- fecal oral, contaminated water or undercooked seafood, only acute, causes fuminant hepatitis in pregnant (massive liver necrosis and failure)
    HBV- parenteral (childbirth, unprotected sex, IVDA), chronic in 20%, acute= HBsAg and IgM-HBcAB, window= IgM-HBcAB, resolved= IgG-HBcAB and IgG-HBsAB, chronic= HBsAg >6mo with HBeAg or HBV DNA (infectivity) and IgG-HBcAB, immunization= IgG-HBsAB
    HCV- parenteral (IVDA, unprotected sex), chronic in most cases, HCV-RNA confirms infx
    HDV- dependent on HBV for infx (superinfx>coinfx), needs viral coating

  • Cirrhosis

    end-stage liver damage, bands of fibrosis (TGFb from stellate cells that line sinusoids), regenerative nodules of hepatocytes
    portal HTN --> ascites, congestive splenomeg, portosystemic shunts (eso varices, hemorrhoids, caput medusae), hepatorenal syndrome
    dec detox --> inc serum ammonia (mental status, asterixis, coma), inc estrogen (gynecomastia, spider angioma, palmar erythema), jaundice
    dec prot synthesis --> hypoalbuminemia (edema), coagulopathy (inc PT, dec clotting factors)

  • EtOH- related liver dz

    damage to parenchyma, fatty liver, alcoholic hepatitis from chemical injury (acetaldehyde, hepatocyte swelling with mallory bodies of damaged cytokeratin, painful hepatomeg, AST>ALT, can lead to cirrhosis (10-20% alcoholics)

  • non-EtOH fatty liver dz

    assn obesity, ALT>AST

  • hemochromatosis

    excess body iron, hemosiderosis (tissue deposition) --> organ damage due to free radicals
    primary= AR defect in absorption (C282Y HFE gene mutation= no absorption regulation), secondary= transfusions
    late adultood
    triad- cirrhosis, secondary DM, bronze skin
    dilated CM, arrhythmia, testicular atrophy possible
    inc ferritin, dec TIBC, inc serum Fe
    prussian blue stain of hepatocytes (lipofuscin is from peroxidized lipids and is common, can confuse for iron)
    inc risk hepatocellular CA
    tx- phlebotomy

  • wilson dz

    AR ATP7B gene defect (Cu transport), no Cu in bile and no Cu into ceruloplasmin, Cu builds up in hepatocytes --> OH free radicals
    childhood
    cirrhosis, neuro sx (behav, dementia, chorea, parkinsonian- Cu deposition in basal ganglia), Kayser-Fleisher rings in cornea
    inc urine Cu, dec serum ceruloplasmin, Cu on liver biopsy
    inc rick hepatocellular CA
    tx- D-penicillamine (chelate)

  • Primary biliary cirrhosis

    AI granulomatous destruction of intrahepatic bile ducts, 40s female, ANTIMITOCHONDRIAL AB, obstructive jaundice

  • primary sclerosing cholangitis

    inflam and fibrossi of intra/extrahepatic bile ducts, periductal fibrosis with onion skin, dilated uninvolved regions
    assn UC and pANCA
    obstructive jaundice, risk cholangioCA

  • reye syndrome

    fulminant liver failure and encephalopathy, aspirin to child with viral infx, mitochondrial damage, hypoglycemia, inc liver enz, N/V, coma/death

  • hepatic adenoma

    benign, assn oral contraceptives, risk of rupture (intraperitoneal bleeding) with pregnancy, subcapsular tumor, grow with estrogen

  • hepatocellular CA

    malignant
    RF= chronic HBV/HCV, cirrhosis, aflatoxins from aspergillus (p53 mut), Budd-Chiari (infarction after hepatic vein obstruction, painful hepatomeg and ascites)
    detected late b/c masked by cirrhosis, poor px
    tumor marker AFP

  • mets to liver

    colon/pancreas/lunc/breast, more common than primary, multiple nodules, hepatomeg with nodular free edge
