  • neoplasia

    tissue growth that is unregulated/irreversible/monoclonal, clonality determined by G6PD isoforms (lyonization on X chr- 1:1 in hyperplasia, alter ratio in neoplasia), clonality of B cells by Ig light chain (nml kappa:lambda 3:1, infx is same ratio, neoplasm is inverted or >6:1), benign (-oma) is localized, malignant (-carcinoma, -sarcoma) invades locally and has potential for mets

  • cancer epidemiology

    2nd MCC death after CV dz/accidents
    MC CA breast/prostate>lung>colorectal
    MCC CA-death lung>breast/prostate>colorectal

  • CA screening

    earliest sx after 30 divisios of mutated cell, more mutations with each division, late detection=poor px
    screen to catch dysplasia before clinical sx
    pap smear- detect CIN before carcinoma
    mammography- detect DCIS before invasion or before clinically palpable
    PSA and DRE- detect prostate carcinoma before spread, peripheral=no urinary sx
    hemoccult test and colonoscopy- detect adenoma before carcinoma, remove adematous polyps to prevent

  • carcinogens

    damage DNA of stem cells, inc risk for CA
    chemicals, oncogenic viruses, radiation
    aflatoxin- moldy grains, aspergillus, hepatocellular CA
    alkylating agents- chemoTx, leukemia/lymphoma
    alcohol- SCC of oropharynx and upper eso, cirrhosis --> hepatocellular CA, pancreatitis --> pancreatic CA
    arsenic- cigarette smoke, SCC of skin/lungCA/angiosarcoma of liver
    asbestos- lung CA > mesothelioma of pleura
    cigarette smoke- polycyclic hydrocarbons, MC carcinogen
    CA of oropharynx/eso/lung/kidney/bladder/pancreas
    Nitrosamines- smoked foods in japan, intestinal type stomach CA
    naphthylamine- cigarette smoke, ureothelial bladder CA
    vinyl chloride- PVC pipes, angiosarcoma of liver
    nickle/chromium/beryllium/silica- occupational, lung CA
    EBV- nasapharyngeal CA, burkitt lymphoma, CNS lymphoma in AIDS
    HHV8- kaposi sarcoma in eastern european men and immunosuppresed pts
    HBV/HCV- hepatocellular CA
    HTLV1- adult T-cell leukemia/lymphoma
    HPV 16/18/31/33- SCC of vulva/vagina/anus/cervix, adenocarcinoma of cervix
    ionizing radiation (nuclear reactor, radioTx)- generates hydroxyl free radicals
    AML/CML/papillary thyroid CA
    nonionizing radiation (UVB)- pyrimidine dimers in DNA (nmly excised by restriction endonucleases)
    basal cell CA, SCC, melanoma

  • Proto-oncogenes

    essential for cell growth/diff, mutation=unregulated growth
    GF, GFr, signal transducers, TFs, cell cycle regulators
    PDGFB- astrocytoma, overexpression, autocrine loop
    ERBB2 (HER2/neu)- EGFr, amplified, breast CA
    RET- neural GFr, pt mutation
    MEN2A/B, sporadic thyroid medullary CA
    KIT- stem cell GFr, pt mutation
    GI stromal tumor
    RAS- pt mutation (dec GTPase activity)
    carcinomas, melanoma, lymphoma
    ABL- TK, philadelphia chr in CML/ALL
    c-MYC- TF, translocation in Burkitt
    N-MYC- amplified in neuroblastoma
    L-MYC- amplified in small cell lung CA
    CCND1 (cyclin D1)- G1-->S, translocated in mantle cell lymphoma
    CDK4- amplified in melanoma

  • tumor suppressor genes

    regulate cell growth, suppress risk of tumor
    p53- rgulates G1-->S, upregulates repair enz when DNA is damaged, induce apoptosis if repair not possible (inc BAX --> disrupt Bcl2 --> cyt C leaks --> caspase cascade)
    Knudson 2 hit hypothesis (must knock out both copies to form tumor)
    found in >50% cancers
    Li-Fraumeni syndrome (germline mut in 1 copy, 2nd hit is somatic, multiple carcinomas/sarcomas)
    Rb- regulates G1-->S, holds E2F TF, phosphorylation by CyclinD/CDK4 complex=release --> E2F allows transition to S phase
    mutation= constitutively free E2F=uncontrolled growth
    Knudson 2 hit hypothesis, sporadic=both somatic (unilateral retinoblastoma), familial=1 germline mutation (bilateral retinoblastoma and osterosarcoma)

  • Apoptosis regulation

    Bcl2 normally blocks cytochrome C release --> prevent apoptosis in nml cells
    follicular lymphoma- overexpressed Bcl2 with t14:18 = apoptosis prohibited

  • telomerase

    telomeres shorten with each division leading to senescence
    upregulated telomerase preserves telomeres --> immortality

  • angiogenesis

    FGF and VEGF cause productin of new BVs

  • avoid immune surveillance

    downregulate MHC1 --> not detected by CD8 cytotoxic cells

  • invasion and spread

    downregulate E-cadherin --> dissociation of attached cells, attach to laminin --> destroy type 4 collagen BM via collagenase
    attach to fibronectin in ECM and spread locally
    vasc/lymphatic allows distant mets

  • metastasis

    lymphatic- carcinomas, spread to regional draining LNs
    Hematogenous- sarcomas, Renal cell CA (renal vein), hepatocellular CA (hepatic vein), follicular thyroid CA, choriocarcinoma
    body cavity seeding- ovarian CA (omental caking of peritoneum)

  • Benign tumors

    slow growing, well circumscribed, distict, mobile
    organized growth, uniform nuclei, low N:C, minimum mitosis, no invasion, no mets potential

  • malignant tumors

    rapid growing, poorly circumscribed, infiltrative, fixed
    lose polarity, nuclear pleomorphism, hyperchromasia, high N:C, high mitosis, invasion through BM or local tissue, METS POTENTIAL
