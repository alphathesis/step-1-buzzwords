  • mumps

    bilateral inflamed parotids
    orchitis, pancreatitis, aseptic meningitis

  • sialadenitis

    unilateral inflammation of salivary gland, obstructing stone leading to staphy aureus infx

  • pleomorphic adenoma

    MC tumor of salivary gland, benign, stromal and epithelial tissue (biphasic), mobile/painless/circumscribed mass at angle of jaw (parotid), high recurrence, transformation into carcinoma = CN 7 signs

  • warthin tumor

    benign cystic tumor, abundant lymphocytes and germinal centers, parotid

  • mucoepidermoid carcinoma

    MC malignant salivary tumor, mucinous and squamous cells, parotid, involves facial nerve

  • tracheoesophageal fistula

    congenital defect, connection btwn esophagus and trachea, proximal asophageal atresia with distal esophagus arising from trachea, vomiting/polyhydramnios/abd distention/aspiration

  • esophageal web

    upper eso protrusion of mucosa, dysphagia, inc risk SCC, plummer-vinson = severe iron deficiency anemia, esophageal web, beefy-red tongue

  • zenker diverticulum

    false diverticulum, outpouching of pharyngeal mucosa, above UES, dysphagia/obstruction/halitosis (bad breath)

  • mallory-weiss syndrome

    longitudinal tears of mucosa at GE junction, severe vomiting (alcoholic, bulemia), painful hematemesis, risk of Boerhaave (eso rupture causing air in mediastinum and subcutaneuous emphysema)

  • esophageal varices

    dilated submucosal veins in lower eso, secondary to portal HTN (left gastric --> eso vein), asymptomatic, rupture risk (painless hematemesis, MCC death in cirrhosis)

  • achalasia

    can't relax LES, damaged ganglion cells in myenteric plexus (btwn inner circular and outer longitudinal MP, idiopathic or trypanosoma cruzi infx in Chagas dz)
    solid and liquid dysphagi, putrid breath, high LES pressure, bird-beak barium swallow, inc risk SCC

  • GERD

    reduced LES tone, RF=EtOH/smoking/obesity/fat-rich diet/caffeine/hiatal hernia (stomach cardia into eso or paraeso = hourglass stomach)
    heartburn, adult asthma, cough, tooth enamel damage, ulceration with stricture, can cause barrett esophagus

  • Barrett esophagus

    metaplasia of lower eso mucosa, nonciliated columnar with goblet cells, acidic stress, may progress to dysplasia and adenoCA

  • esophageal adenocarcinoma

    prolif of glands, MC in the west, from preexisting barrett eso, lower 1/3 of eso, LN- lower celiac and gastric

  • esophageal SCC

    MC worldwide, upper/middle third of eso, RF=ETOH/smoking/hot tea/achalasia/esophageal web/lye, poor Px
    progressive dysphagia, weight loss, pain, hematemesis, hoarseness (recurrent laryngeal), cough (tracheal)
    LN- upper cervical, middle mediastinal/tracheobronchial

  • Gastroschisis

    congenital malformation of anterior wall, exposure of contents

  • omphalocele

    herniation of bowel in umbilical cord, failure of herniated intestines to return to body cavity, covered by peritoneum and amnion of umbilicle cord

  • pyloric stenosis

    congenital hypertrophy of pyloric SM, M>F, two weeks after birth
    nonbilious vomiting, visible peristalsis, olive-like mass in abdomen
    tx- myotomy

  • acute gastritis

    acidic damage due to imbalance
    defense= foveolar mucin layer, epithelium bicarb secretion, blood supply
    RF= severe burn (curling ulcer, hypovolemia --> dec blood supply), NSAIDS, EtOH, chemoTx, inc intracranial pressure (cushing ulcer- inc vagus stimulation, inc acid), shock (stress ulcers)
    superficial inflammation, erosion (lose epithelium), ulcer (lose mucosal)

  • chronic autoimmune gastritis

    Ab against parietal cells/intrinsic factor, T cells (type 4 HSR), mucosal atrophy with intestinal metaplasia (goblet cells), achlorhydria with inc gastrin and antral G-cell hyperplasia, megaloblastic pernicious anemia, risk of intestinal type gastric adenocarcinoma

  • chronic H.pylori gastritis

    MC gastritis, ureases and proteases weaken mucosal defenses, antrum, epigastric abd pain, inc risk ulceration/intestinal type gastric adenocarcinoma/MALT marginal xone lymphoma
    tx- triple therapy, neg urea breath test, lack of stool Ag

  • Peptic ulcer dz- duodenal

    90%, proximal, 95% from H.pylori/5% from zollinger ellison gastrinoma, epigastric pain that improves with meals, ulcer with brunner gland hypertrophy, usually anterior duodenum, posterior duodenum has risk of gastroduodenal artery bleeding or acute pancreatitis, almost never malignant

  • Peptic ulcer dz- gastric

    75% from H.pylori/others from NSAID and bile reflux, epigastric pain that worsens with meals, lesser curvature of antrum, risk of bleeding from left gastric artery, can be from intestinal type gastric carcinoma (large, irregular, heaped up margins), benign=small, sharply demarcated, radiating folds of mucosa

  • gastric carcinoma

    presents late
    weight loss, abd pain, anemia, early satiety
    acanthosis nigricans or leser-trelat sign (lots of SKs on skin)
    LN spread to left supraclavicular Virchow node

  • Intestinal type gastric carcinoma

    large irregular ulser with heaped up margins, lesser curvature of antrum
    RF= intestinal metaplasia (AI gastritis, H.pylori), nitrosamines in smoked japanese food, blood type A
    mets- sister mary joseph nodules in periumbilical region

  • diffuse type gastric carcinoma

    signet ring cells, desmoplasia causes stomach wall thickening (linitis plastica), mets- krukenburg tumor in bilateral ovaries

  • duodenal atresia

    assn down syndrome, congenital failure to canalize
    polyhydramnios, stomach distention and blind loop (double bubble), bilious vomiting

  • meckel diverticulum

    true, outpouching of all 3 layers of bowel wall, failure of vitelline duct to involute
    2% population, 2 inches long withing 2 feet of ileocecal valve, presents in first 2 yrs of life with bleeding/volvulus/intessusception/obstruction

  • volvulus

    twisting of bowel along mesentery, obstruction, distruption of blood supply, MC sigmoid colon (elderly) and cecum (YA)

  • intussusception

    telescoping of proximal segment forward into distal segment, pulled fwd by peristalsis, obstruction and disruption of blood supply with infarction
    children- rotavirus lymphoid hyperplasia (peyer's patches), terminal ileum, intussusception into cecum
    adults- MCC tumor

  • small bowel infarction

    transmural infarction with thrombosis/embolism of SMA (PAN, A. fib) or thrombosis of mesenteric vein
    mucosal infarction with marked hypoTN
    abd pain, bloody diarrhea, dec bowel sounds

  • lactose intolerance

    dec function of brush border lactase
    abd distenstion and diarrhesa with mild consumption (osmotic diarrhea)
    congenital is AR
    acquired develops in late childhood
    temporary deficiency after infx
    nml histology of bowel

  • celiac Dz

    immune damage of small bowel villi due to gluten exposure (gliadin- deamidated by tTG, presented in MHC II, CD4 cells mediate damage)
    abd distension, diarrhea, failure to thrive, bloating, dermatitis herpetiformis from IgA deposition in dermal papillae tips
    flattening of villi, crypt hyperplasia, inc intraepithelial lymphocytes, mostly duodenum
    sx resolve w/ gluten free diet
    can lead to small bowl carcinoma or T-cell lymphoma

  • tropical sprue

    malabsorption, after infx diarrhea, responds to abx, mostly jejunum/ileum, similar to celiac

  • whipple dz

    macs loaded with tropheryma whippeli (PAS-positive), small bowel lamina propria (macs compress lacteals, chylomicrons not transferred, fat malabsorption, steatorrhea)
    also joints, cardiac valves, LNs, CNS

  • abetalipoproteinemia

    AR deficiency of apolipoprotein B-48 and B-100, defective chylomicron formation, absent VLDL and LDL

  • carcinoid tumor

    neuroendocrine cells, chromogranin, small bowel MC site, submucosal polyp-like nodule, secretes SEROTONIN (metabolized to 5-HIAA by MAO in liver, metastatis leasts to carcinoid syndrome with bronchospasm/diarrhea/flushing and carcinoid heart dz with tricuspid regurg and PV stenosis), lungs have MAO to metabolize serotonin so no LH dz

  • acute appendicitis

    MCC acute abdomen, obstruction from lymphoid hyperplasia or fecalith, infx follows
    periumbilical pain, fever, nausea, McBurney point tenderness, rupture causes pertonitis with guarding and rebound tenderness, periappendiceal abscess risk

  • inflammatory bowel dz

    chronic, relapsing, young women, recurrent bloody diarrhea and abd pain, dx of exclusion

  • ulcerative colitis

    mucosal and submucosal ulcers, begins in rectums and ascends, continuous, LLQ pain (rectum), bloody diarrhea, crypt abscesses with neutrophils, pseudopolyps with loss of haustra (lead pipe), risk of toxic megacolon and carcinoma (>10 yrs)
    assn- primary sclerosing cholangitis, p-ANCA, ankylosing spondylitis, sacroilitis, migratory polyarthritis
    smoking protective

  • crohn dz

    full-thickness inflammation with knife-like fissures, skip lesions, terminal ileum MC site, rectum least common, RLQ pain (ileum) with non-bloody diarrhea, lymphoid aggregates with granulomas, cobblestone mucosa, creeping fat (string sign), strictures (fibrosis)
    risk of malabsorption, calcium oxalate nepthrolithiasis, fistula formation, carcinoma if colonic
    assn- erythema nodosum, uveitis
    smoking inc risk

  • Hirschsprung Dz

    defective relaxation and peristalsis of rectum and distal sigmoid colon, assn down syndrome, congenital failure of neural crest ganglion cell descent into myentetic and submucosal plexus
    failure to pass meconium, empty rectal vault, megacolon proximal to obstruction
    rectal suction biopsy
    tx- resect involved bowel

  • colonic diverticula

    false diverticula, outpouching of mucosa and submucosa, usually L side- sigmoid colon MC site (vasa reta transverse muscularis propria), assn with constipation/straining/low-fiber diet, older adults, asymptomatia
    risk of hematochezia (bright red), diverticulitis (appendicitis -like sx in LLQ), colovesicular fistula (to bladder- air/stool in urine)

  • angiodysplasia

    malformation of mucosal/submucosal capillary beds, usually R side- cecum and right colon, rupture=hematochezia in elderly

  • hereditary hemorrhagic telangiectasia

    AD dz with thin-walled BVs, mouth and GI tract, bleeding

  • ischemic colitis

    splenic flexure (watershed of SMA), MCC atherosclerosis of SMA, postprandial pain, weight loss, infarction= pain and bloody diarrhea

  • irritable bowel syndrome

    relapsing pain with bloating/flatulence/constipation/diarrhea that improves with defecation, middle-age females
    disturbed motility but no identifiable change, inc fiber improves sx

  • hyperplastic colonic polyps

    hyperplasia of glands, serrated appearance, MC type, left colon (rectosigmoid), benign with no malignant potential

  • adenomatous colonic polyp

    neoplastic proliferation of glands, premalignant, can progress to adenocarcinoma (APC --> K-ras --> p53)
    aspirin impreds progression
    remove during colonoscopy
    inc risk of progression if >2 cm sessile and villous

  • familial adenomatous polyposis (FAP)

    AD dz with adenomatous colonic polyps, inhertited APC mutaution on chr 5, colon and rectum removed prophylactically

  • gardner syndrome

    FAP with fibromatosis in retroperitoneum and osteomas in skull

  • turcot syndrome

    FAP with CNS turmors (medulloblastoma, glial)

  • juvenile polyp

    sporatic, benign, kids <5y/o, solitary polyp that prolapses and bleeds
    juvenile polyposis= multiple juvenile polyps in stomach and colon, inc risk progression to carcinoma

  • Peutz-Jeghers syndrome

    benign polyps throughout GI tract, hyperpigmentation on lips/oral mucosa/genital skin
    AD
    inc risk colorectal/breast/gynecologic cancer

  • colorectal carcinoma

    3rd MC CA, 3rd MC CA death
    60-70 y/o, adenoma-carcinoma sequence (adenomatous polyp) or microsatellite instability (DNA mismatch repair enzyme mutation = HNPCC)
    colonoscopy and fecal occult blood test at 50 y/o to screen
    L= napkin-ring, dec stool caliber, LLQ pain, blood-streaked stool
    R= raised lesion, iron-deficient anemia, occult bleeding, bague pain
    inc risk strep bovis endocarditis
    CEA is tumor marker for tx response and recurrence (not screening)

  • lynch syndrome

    HNPCC, ovarian, endometrial carcinomas
    colorectal- not from adenomatous polyps (de novo), early age, R-sided
