  • Breast structure

    modified sweat gland derived from skin, can develop anywhere along milk line from axilla to vulva (supernumerary nipples), lobules make milk and ducts drain to the nipple, 2-layer epithelium (inner luminal makes milk, outer myoepithelial contracts to propel milk)
    hormone sensitive (before puberty mainly large ducts, ER and PR cause lobule/small duct development in puberty and hyperplasia in pregnancy, atrophy after menopause), highest density in UOQ

  • galactorrhea

    milk production outside of lactation, not a sx of BrCA
    due to nipple stimulation, prolactinoma, drugs

  • acute mastitis

    bacterial infx, staph aureus, assn breast feeding, erythematous breast with purulent discharge, continue feeding and dicloxacillin

  • periductal mastitis

    inflam of subareolar ducts in SMOKERS, vit A deficiency causes squamous metaplasia of ducts --> blockage --> inflam proximal to block
    subareolar mass with nipple retraction

  • mammary duct ectasia

    inflam with subareolar duct dilation, multiparous postmenopausal women, periareolar mass with GREEN-BROWN DISCHARGE, biopsy shows plasma cells

  • fat necrosis

    usu assn with trauma, abnl calcification on mammography (saponification), biopsy shows necrotic fat with calcifications and giant cells

  • fibrocystic change

    benign, MC change in premenopausal, fibrosis and cysts, hormone-mediated, vague lumps in UOQ, blue-dome cysts
    fibrosis, cysts, apocrine metaplasia= no inc risk for invasive CA
    ductal hyperplasia and sclerosing adenosis (inc # glands with fibrosis, often calcified)= 2x inc risk (both breasts)
    atypical hyperplasia= 5x inc risk (both breasts)

  • Intraductal papilloma

    benign, papillary growth into large duct, fibrovascular projection with luminal and myoepithelial lining, premenopausal, BLOODY discharge

  • Fibroadenoma

    MC benign tumor in premenopausal, fibrous tissue and glands, well-circumscribed mobile mass, ER-sensitive, no inc risk of CA

  • Phyllodes tumor

    mostly benign (can be malignant), overgrowth of fibrous component = leaf-life projection, postmenopausal

  • breast cancer

    MC CA in women, 2nd MC CA death
    RF= estrogen (female, postmenopausal, early menarche, late menopause, obesity, atypical hyperplasia, 1st-degree relative)

  • papillary carcinoma

    malignant, fibrovascular projection with luminal lining (NO MYOEPITHELIAL), postmenopausal, bloody discharge

  • DCIS

    malignant, no BM invasion, detected as calcification on mammogram- must biopsy, no mass,
    comedo type- high grade, central duct necrosis and dystrophic calcification
    paget dz- DCIS that extends to involve skin of nipple (nipple ulceration and erythema), almost always assn with underyling CA

  • Invasive ductal CA

    forms duct-like structures, MC type of BrCA, mass detected by physical exam (>2cm) or mammogram (>1cm), advanced tumors cause skin dimpling or nipple retraction
    biopsy shows duct-like structures in DESMOPLASTIC STROMA
    tubular CA- well diff tubules that LACK MYOEPITHELIAL cells, good px
    mucinous CA- abundant extracellular mucin, older women, good px
    medullary CA- large high-grade cells, lymphocytes and plasma cells, well-circumscribed mass like fibroadenoma, inc risk in BRCA1 carrier, good px
    inflammatory CA- in dermal lymphatics, inflamed/swollen breast, no discrete mass, mistaken for acute mastitis (abx don't improve in 5-10 days), poor px

  • lobular CIS

    malignant, cells in lobules, no BM invasion, no mass or calcification (discover incidentally on biopsy), dyscohesive cells LACKING E-CADHERIN ADHESION PROTEIN, multifocal and bilateral, tx- tamoxifen, low risk of progression to invasive

  • invasive lobular CA

    cells grow in SINGLE-FILE PATTERN, may have signet-ring, NO DUCT FORMATION

  • hereditary BrCA

    10% of BrCA
    multiple 1st-degree, premenopausal, multiple tumors
    BRCA1 and BRCA2 mutations
    prophylactic bilateral mastectomy dec risk but still small risk in axilla and chest wall subcutaneous tissue

  • male BrCA

    1% of BrCA
    assn BRCA2 and klinefelter
    subareolar mass in older males (highest density under nipple), nipple discharge

  • BrCA Px

    Px based on TNM staging (M most important but N most useful b/c most don't have mets at presentation), sentinel LN biopsy to take minimal nodes while removing affected ones reliably

  • BrCA Tx Prediction

    ER+ PR+ respond to antiestrogen tx (tamoxifen), nuclear rcptr
    HER2/neu+ respond to trastuzumab/herceptin, cell surface rcptr
    triple negative (AA wome) have poor px
